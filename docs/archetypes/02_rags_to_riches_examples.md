# Rags to Riches — Example Mapping

**Primary example:** Rocky (1976 film)
**Cross-references:** Slumdog Millionaire (2008 film), Great Expectations (1861 novel)

---

## Node Mappings

| Node ID | Node Label | Rocky Moment |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| RR_N01 | Lowly State | Rocky Balboa is a small-time boxer and debt collector in Philadelphia. He has talent but no opportunity, living in a run-down apartment with no prospects. |
| RR_N02 | Opportunity Appears | Apollo Creed's scheduled opponent drops out. Creed picks Rocky as a replacement — a nobody with a good story — purely as a publicity stunt. |
| RR_N03 | Initial Rise | Rocky enters the world of big-time boxing: media attention, a real trainer (Mickey), training facilities, public recognition. |
| RR_N04 | Social Trials | The media treats Rocky as a joke. Mickey initially refuses to train him. Rocky doubts whether he belongs and struggles with the brutal training regimen. |
| RR_N05 | Apparent Peak | Rocky completes his training. He has the confidence, the support of Adrian, and the physical preparation. He is as ready as he will ever be. |
| RR_N06 | Crisis of Worth | The fight itself. Rocky must prove he is not just a publicity stunt. The question is not whether he wins but whether he belongs in the ring with the champion. |
| RR_N07 | Proof of Character | Rocky goes the full 15 rounds — the first person to do so against Apollo. He fights with heart, skill, and endurance that prove his quality beyond doubt. |
| RR_N08 | True Elevation | Rocky loses the decision but wins the moral victory. He has Adrian, self-respect, and the knowledge that he went the distance. His elevation is in character, not title. |

**Slumdog Millionaire cross-reference:** Jamal's lowly state is the Mumbai slums; each quiz show question triggers a flashback that maps to a trial; the crisis is the accusation of cheating; the proof is that his life experience (not luck) gave him every answer; true elevation is reunion with Latika.

**Great Expectations cross-reference:** Pip's lowly state is the forge; the opportunity is the anonymous benefactor; social trials are London society; the crisis is learning the benefactor is Magwitch, not Miss Havisham; proof of character is Pip's loyalty to Magwitch despite disgrace.

## Edge Mappings

| Edge ID | Edge Label | Rocky Moment |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| RR_E01 | Chance Arrives | Apollo's team picks Rocky's name from a list. The opportunity is random — but Rocky's character will prove it wasn't wasted. |
| RR_E02 | Seizes the Chance | Rocky accepts the fight despite knowing he's outmatched. He commits to training. |
| RR_E03 | New World Pushes Back | Mickey's initial rejection, media mockery, the gap between Rocky's world and the championship stage. |
| RR_E04 | Reaches the Top | The training montage culminates with Rocky running up the Philadelphia Museum steps — he has reached his physical and emotional peak. |
| RR_E05 | Foundation Cracks | The night before the fight, Rocky confesses to Adrian that he can't win. The foundation of confidence cracks — but he redefines his goal: go the distance. |
| RR_E06 | Chooses Character Over Status | Rocky fights not to win the title but to prove he's not "just another bum from the neighborhood." He chooses character over the impossible goal of victory. |
| RR_E07 | Earns True Place | The final bell. Rocky has gone 15 rounds. He doesn't care about the judges' decision — he calls for Adrian. His place is earned through endurance and heart. |
