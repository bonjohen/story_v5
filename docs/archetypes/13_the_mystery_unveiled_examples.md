# The Mystery Unveiled — Example Mapping

**Primary example:** Knives Out (2019 film)
**Cross-references:** The Sixth Sense (1999 film), Gone Girl (2012 novel)

---

## Node Mappings

| Node ID | Node Label | Knives Out Moment |
|---------|-----------|------------------|
| MU_N01 | Surface Order | The Thrombey family gathers for patriarch Harlan Thrombey's 85th birthday. The family appears united but is riven by entitlement, resentment, and financial dependence. Harlan is found dead the next morning — apparent suicide. |
| MU_N02 | Central Question Posed | Detective Blanc is anonymously hired to investigate. He announces that the death is not what it appears. The question: was Harlan murdered, and if so, by whom? Every family member had motive — Harlan was cutting them all off. |
| MU_N03 | Initial Clues | Blanc interviews the family. Each member has an alibi that subtly contradicts the others. Marta, Harlan's nurse, appears to be the only honest witness. Physical evidence (the blood on her shoe, the toxicology) points in confusing directions. The audience knows more than Blanc — we see Marta accidentally switching Harlan's medications. |
| MU_N04 | False Trail | The investigation initially points toward Marta as the accidental killer — she believes she administered a fatal overdose of morphine. Marta and the audience follow this trail: she is guilty, the question is only whether she'll be caught. Ransom Drysdale appears to help her cover up. |
| MU_N05 | Deepening Complexity | The toxicology report reveals no morphine in Harlan's system — Marta did not make the mistake she thought she did. Someone switched the medication labels to frame her. The mystery deepens: who set the trap, and why? Harlan's new will, leaving everything to Marta, makes every family member a suspect with intensified motive. The crime is not accidental — it's a conspiracy. |
| MU_N06 | Key Breakthrough | Blanc assembles the evidence: Ransom knew about the will change, had access to the medications, and has been manipulating Marta's guilt to control her. The "help" Ransom offered was actually maneuvering to ensure the original will (benefiting him) would be restored through Marta's self-destruction. |
| MU_N07 | Confrontation with Truth | Blanc gathers the family and reveals Ransom's scheme in full: Ransom switched the medication labels hoping Marta would kill Harlan; when she didn't (because her training overrode the labels), Ransom improvised by killing the housekeeper Fran and attempting to frame Marta. Ransom lunges at Marta with a knife (retractable — from Harlan's prop collection). His guilt is confirmed. |
| MU_N08 | New Understanding | Marta stands on the balcony of the Thrombey mansion — now legally hers — holding her coffee mug that reads "My House, My Rules, My Coffee." The family stands below, dispossessed. The mystery revealed not just a murder plot but the moral bankruptcy of a family that treated their patriarch's generosity as entitlement. Justice is served, but the family is destroyed. |

**The Sixth Sense cross-reference:** Surface order is Malcolm Crowe's apparent recovery from the shooting; the question is what is wrong with Cole Sear; initial clues are Cole's terror and social isolation; the false trail is the assumption that Cole is mentally ill; deepening is the discovery that Cole genuinely sees dead people; the breakthrough is Cole's advice to Malcolm — "They don't know they're dead"; the confrontation with truth is Malcolm's realization that he himself is dead; new understanding reframes every scene in the film.

**Gone Girl cross-reference:** Surface order is the Dunnes' apparently normal marriage; the question is who killed Amy; initial clues point to Nick as the murderer; the false trail is the diary (planted by Amy); deepening reveals Amy is alive and orchestrating her own disappearance as revenge; the breakthrough is Nick's discovery of Amy's scheme; the confrontation is Amy's return and Nick's trapped compliance; new understanding is the horror that the "perfect marriage" is now a permanent prison of mutual blackmail.

## Edge Mappings

| Edge ID | Edge Label | Knives Out Moment |
|---------|-----------|------------------|
| MU_E01 | Question Emerges | Blanc's arrival and announcement that the death is suspicious. The comfortable story of suicide is challenged. Every family member becomes a suspect. |
| MU_E02 | Gathers First Clues | The family interviews. Each Thrombey tells a version of the evening that serves their interests. Marta's involuntary vomiting when she lies makes her the only reliable witness. The contradictions between accounts are the first real clues. |
| MU_E03 | Follows the Wrong Lead | Marta's belief that she killed Harlan. The audience follows this trail — we saw her switch the vials. The investigation appears to be about concealing an accident, not solving a murder. |
| MU_E04 | Reorients the Investigation | The toxicology report shows no morphine overdose. Marta's "mistake" was not a mistake — the labels were switched, but her muscle memory gave the correct dosage. The mystery transforms: from "will Marta be caught?" to "who set the trap?" |
| MU_E05 | Connects the Dots | Blanc traces Ransom's access, motive, and movements. The destroyed blood evidence, Fran's poisoning, and Ransom's "helpful" behavior all converge. Blanc sees the full picture: Ransom orchestrated everything. |
| MU_E06 | Forces the Truth Into the Open | The drawing room reveal. Blanc lays out the scheme before the entire family. Ransom's attack on Marta with the retractable knife is the final confirmation — he exposes himself in front of witnesses. |
| MU_E07 | Truth Settles | Marta inherits the estate. The family's entitlement is exposed. Ransom is arrested. The mystery's resolution is both a whodunit answer and a moral judgment: Marta's goodness is rewarded; the Thrombeys' grasping is punished. |
