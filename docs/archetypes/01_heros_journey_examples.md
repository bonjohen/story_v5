# The Hero's Journey — Example Mapping

**Primary example:** Star Wars: Episode IV – A New Hope (1977 film)
**Cross-references:** The Hobbit (1937 novel), The Matrix (1999 film)

---

## Node Mappings

| Node ID | Node Label | Star Wars Moment |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| HJ_N01 | Ordinary World | Luke farms moisture on Tatooine, longing for adventure while fulfilling obligations to Uncle Owen. |
| HJ_N02 | Call to Adventure | R2-D2 plays Princess Leia's holographic message; Obi-Wan Kenobi asks Luke to come to Alderaan and learn the Force. |
| HJ_N50 | Refusal of the Call | Luke declines — "I can't get involved. I've got work to do." His duty to his uncle overrides the call. |
| HJ_N03 | Meeting the Mentor | Obi-Wan trains Luke in the Force during the journey to Mos Eisley, gives him his father's lightsaber, and tells him the (partial) truth about Anakin. |
| HJ_N04 | Crossing the Threshold | Luke leaves Tatooine aboard the Millennium Falcon after discovering the Empire has killed his aunt and uncle. There is nothing to return to. |
| HJ_N05 | Road of Trials | The cantina confrontation, the garbage compactor, blaster training aboard the Falcon, evading TIE fighters, learning the ways of the Force. |
| HJ_N06 | Approach to the Inmost Cave | The Falcon is captured by the Death Star's tractor beam. The group enters the enemy's fortress. |
| HJ_N07 | The Ordeal | Rescuing Leia from the detention block; Obi-Wan's duel with Darth Vader and his death. Luke loses his mentor — the deepest personal cost of the journey. |
| HJ_N08 | Reward | The Death Star plans are secured. Luke has begun to trust the Force. He has Leia, Han, and Chewie as allies. |
| HJ_N09 | The Road Back | The Rebels prepare the assault on the Death Star. Luke must commit to the fight rather than grieve. |
| HJ_N10 | Resurrection | The trench run. Luke turns off his targeting computer and trusts the Force — proving his transformation is real under ultimate pressure. He destroys the Death Star. |
| HJ_N11 | Return with the Elixir | Medal ceremony. Luke is integrated into the Rebel community as a hero. The ordinary farmboy is gone; the Jedi-in-training has arrived. |

**The Hobbit cross-reference:** Bilbo's ordinary world is the Shire; his call is Gandalf's invitation; the threshold is leaving the Shire; the ordeal is the confrontation with Smaug; the elixir is the Ring and the treasure he brings home.

**The Matrix cross-reference:** Neo's ordinary world is his cubicle; the call is "Follow the white rabbit"; the mentor is Morpheus; the threshold is choosing the red pill; the ordeal is the betrayal by Cypher and the Agent Smith fight; the resurrection is Neo's death and rebirth as "The One."

## Edge Mappings

| Edge ID | Edge Label | Star Wars Moment |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| HJ_E01 | Call Received | Leia's message disrupts Luke's routine — the wider galactic conflict intrudes on his small world. |
| HJ_E50 | Hero Refuses | Luke tells Obi-Wan he can't leave — his obligations hold him. The delay is credible and character-driven. |
| HJ_E51 | Refusal Overridden | The Empire kills Owen and Beru. Luke's reason to stay is destroyed. The stakes have worsened because of the delay. |
| HJ_E02 | Seeks Guidance | Luke returns to Obi-Wan with nothing left to lose. He asks to learn the ways of the Force. |
| HJ_E03 | Crosses into Special World | Boarding the Falcon and leaving Tatooine. The Stormtrooper chase in Mos Eisley makes the crossing physically dangerous and irreversible. |
| HJ_E04 | Enters Trials | Immediately upon entering hyperspace, Luke begins training with the remote. Trials are continuous from this point. |
| HJ_E05 | Approaches the Core | The Falcon exits hyperspace where Alderaan should be — and finds the Death Star instead. The group is pulled into the enemy's seat of power. |
| HJ_E06 | Faces the Ordeal | The decision to rescue Leia launches the group into the Death Star's interior. Obi-Wan confronts Vader, knowing he will not survive. |
| HJ_E07 | Claims the Reward | Escaping the Death Star with Leia and the plans. The cost (Obi-Wan's death) makes the reward bittersweet. |
| HJ_E08 | Begins Return | Arriving at the Rebel base. The plans are delivered but the Death Star is approaching — the clock is ticking. |
| HJ_E09 | Final Test | The trench run itself — Vader is on Luke's tail, Biggs is killed, R2 is hit. Luke must choose between the targeting computer and the Force. |
| HJ_E10 | Returns Transformed | The Death Star is destroyed. Luke returns to the base as a changed person — from farmboy to hero, from skeptic to Force-user. |
