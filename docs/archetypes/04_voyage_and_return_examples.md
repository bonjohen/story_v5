# Voyage and Return — Example Mapping

**Primary example:** Alice's Adventures in Wonderland (1865 novel)
**Cross-references:** The Wizard of Oz (1939 film), The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe (1950 novel)

---

## Node Mappings

| Node ID | Node Label | Alice in Wonderland Moment |
|---------|-----------|--------------------------|
| VR_N01 | Familiar World | Alice sits on a riverbank with her sister, bored and drowsy. The ordinary world is safe but stifling. |
| VR_N02 | Passage Into the Unknown | Alice follows the White Rabbit down the rabbit hole, falling into Wonderland. The transition is sudden and disorienting. |
| VR_N03 | Fascination Phase | Alice explores Wonderland with curiosity — shrinking, growing, meeting the Cheshire Cat, attending the Mad Tea Party. The world is absurd but delightful. |
| VR_N04 | Shadow Emerges | The Queen of Hearts reveals Wonderland's tyrannical side — arbitrary rules, constant threats of execution, a rigged court system. |
| VR_N05 | Frustration and Imprisonment | Alice is trapped in the Queen's court, subject to nonsensical justice. She cannot reason her way out; Wonderland's rules work against her. |
| VR_N06 | Thrilling Escape | Alice defies the court, declaring "You're nothing but a pack of cards!" She breaks the spell by refusing to accept Wonderland's authority. |
| VR_N07 | Return Changed | Alice wakes by the riverbank. The voyage may have been a dream, but the reader understands she has encountered the absurdity that underlies social convention. |

**Wizard of Oz cross-reference:** Familiar world is Kansas; passage is the tornado; fascination is Munchkinland and the Yellow Brick Road; shadow is the Wicked Witch; frustration is the Witch's castle; escape is the melting; return is "There's no place like home."

**Narnia cross-reference:** Familiar world is wartime England; passage is the wardrobe; fascination is Narnia's wonder; shadow is the White Witch's eternal winter; escape is Aslan's return and the battle; return is stepping back through the wardrobe as children again.

## Edge Mappings

| Edge ID | Edge Label | Alice in Wonderland Moment |
|---------|-----------|--------------------------|
| VR_E01 | Crosses Over | Alice follows the White Rabbit on impulse. The fall is sudden — she has no plan and no way back. |
| VR_E02 | Begins Exploring | Alice encounters each new absurdity with curiosity rather than fear — eating the cake, drinking the potion, talking to the caterpillar. |
| VR_E03 | Darkness Appears | The shift from the whimsical tea party to the Queen's garden. "Off with her head!" signals that the world's absurdity has a violent edge. |
| VR_E04 | Becomes Trapped | Alice enters the trial scene. She cannot leave, cannot reason with the court, and cannot apply familiar logic. |
| VR_E05 | Breaks Free | Alice's growth throughout Wonderland culminates in her refusal to accept the court's authority. Her defiance is the escape. |
| VR_E06 | Returns Home | Waking on the riverbank. The return is quiet — but Alice has experienced the gap between appearance and reality. |
