# The Escape — Example Mapping

**Primary example:** The Shawshank Redemption (1994 film)
**Cross-references:** Escape from Alcatraz (1979 film), Get Out (2017 film)

---

## Node Mappings

| Node ID | Node Label | The Shawshank Redemption Moment |
|---------|-----------|-------------------------------|
| ES_N01 | Confinement | Andy Dufresne is convicted of murdering his wife and her lover — a crime he did not commit — and sentenced to two consecutive life terms at Shawshank State Penitentiary. The prison is brutal: Warden Norton is corrupt, the guards are violent, and the Sisters prey on vulnerable inmates. |
| ES_N02 | Spark of Hope | Andy's friendship with Red and his discovery that the prison's walls are soft, crumbly rock. His background in banking and geology gives him knowledge others lack. The Rita Hayworth poster becomes both symbol and tool. |
| ES_N03 | Planning the Escape | Over nearly two decades, Andy chips a tunnel through his cell wall using a rock hammer small enough to hide in a Bible. Simultaneously, he launders money for Warden Norton, creating a paper trail and a fictitious identity — Randall Stephens — who exists outside the prison with a bank account and social security number. |
| ES_N04 | Setbacks and Near-Discovery | Tommy Williams arrives and reveals that another inmate confessed to Andy's wife's murder — proof of Andy's innocence. Norton has Tommy killed to keep Andy (and his financial services) imprisoned. The one legitimate path to freedom is destroyed by the system itself. |
| ES_N05 | Point of No Return | After Tommy's murder, Andy knows Norton will never release him. The next night, Andy takes Norton's ledger, his shoes, and a set of clothes. He enters the tunnel. There is no going back — if caught, he faces the hole for the rest of his life. |
| ES_N06 | The Escape Attempt | Andy crawls through the tunnel, breaks through the outer wall, and then crawls through five hundred yards of raw sewage pipe to emerge in a creek beyond the prison perimeter. The escape is physical, disgusting, and triumphant. He mails Norton's financial records to the newspaper. |
| ES_N07 | Freedom | Andy surfaces in Zihuatanejo, Mexico, fixing up an old boat on the Pacific. Red, paroled, follows the instructions Andy left and crosses the border. The final image is two friends on the beach — free, but only after decades of confinement that can never be undone. |
| ES_N50 | Recapture (variant) | Not exercised in The Shawshank Redemption — Andy's escape succeeds on the first attempt. In the Escape from Alcatraz cross-reference, recapture is the structural possibility the film leaves ambiguous: the men may have drowned. In Get Out, the variant maps to Chris being momentarily recaptured during the fight — knocked down before fighting free again. |

**Escape from Alcatraz cross-reference:** Confinement is Alcatraz's maximum-security fortress; the spark of hope is discovering the crumbling ventilation grill; planning involves papier-mâché dummy heads and a raft built from raincoats; setbacks include cell inspections and a co-conspirator's claustrophobia; the point of no return is the night of the escape; the attempt is the climb through utility corridors to the roof and across the water; freedom is ambiguous — the men were never found.

**Get Out cross-reference:** Confinement is the Armitage family's estate and the psychological trap of "the sunken place"; the spark of hope is Chris's growing unease and Rod's warnings; planning is Chris's realization of the hypnosis trigger (cotton in the ears); setbacks include the auction and the discovery of the truth; the point of no return is Chris being strapped into the chair; the escape attempt is fighting through each family member; freedom is Rod's arrival — escape from a system designed to consume him.

## Edge Mappings

| Edge ID | Edge Label | The Shawshank Redemption Moment |
|---------|-----------|-------------------------------|
| ES_E01 | Hope Appears | The rock hammer and the soft prison walls. Andy's banking knowledge gives him leverage with Norton, which buys time and cover. The poster hides the growing hole. Hope is material and incremental. |
| ES_E02 | Begins Planning | Andy starts chipping — a process that will take nineteen years. Simultaneously, he creates the Randall Stephens identity. The plan has two tracks: physical escape and financial escape. |
| ES_E03 | Obstacles Arise | Norton's corruption makes Andy indispensable, trapping him further. Tommy's murder eliminates the legal path to freedom. The system escalates its grip precisely because Andy is useful to it. |
| ES_E04 | Now or Never | Tommy's death proves Norton will never let Andy go. The financial records are the last weapon. Andy must act before Norton discovers the tunnel or realizes Andy knows too much. |
| ES_E05 | Executes the Plan | The crawl through the sewage pipe. The escape is not elegant — it is five hundred yards of filth and darkness. Everything Andy built over nineteen years converges into one night. |
| ES_E06 | Breaks Free | Andy emerges in the creek, arms raised in the rain. The next morning, he visits bank after bank as Randall Stephens, withdrawing Norton's laundered money. Norton's corruption is mailed to the press. Freedom is total — financial, physical, and moral. |
| ES_E50 | Caught (variant) | Not exercised in The Shawshank Redemption. In the Get Out cross-reference, the moment Chris is knocked down during the escape sequence before regaining the upper hand maps to the recapture variant — a momentary failure before the final break. |
| ES_E51 | Tries Again (variant) | Not exercised in The Shawshank Redemption. In Get Out, Chris recovers from each setback during the escape and re-engages each new obstacle (Missy, Jeremy, Georgina, Jim Hudson) with escalating stakes — each retry costs more and the margin for error shrinks. |
