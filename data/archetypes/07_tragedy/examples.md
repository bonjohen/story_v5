# Tragedy — Example Mapping

**Primary example:** Macbeth (Shakespeare tragedy)
**Cross-references:** Titanic (1997 film), Romeo and Juliet (Shakespeare tragedy)

---

## Node Mappings

| Node ID | Node Label | Macbeth Moment |
|---------|-----------|---------------|
| TR_N01 | Elevated State | Macbeth is Thane of Glamis, a celebrated warrior who has just won a decisive battle for King Duncan. He is admired, trusted, and honored. His flaw — ambition — is latent. |
| TR_N02 | Temptation or Pressure | The three witches prophesy that Macbeth will be Thane of Cawdor and then King. When the first prophecy comes true immediately, ambition ignites. Lady Macbeth amplifies the temptation, questioning his manhood and resolve. |
| TR_N03 | Fatal Choice | Macbeth murders King Duncan in his sleep — a guest in Macbeth's own castle. The transgression violates every code of honor, hospitality, and loyalty. Macbeth chooses the act deliberately despite his own horror. |
| TR_N04 | Apparent Success | Macbeth is crowned King of Scotland. The plan has worked — Duncan's sons flee, suspicion is deflected, and Macbeth holds the throne. But the crown sits uneasily from the start. |
| TR_N05 | Unraveling | Macbeth orders the murder of Banquo (whose descendants the witches said would be kings). Banquo is killed but Fleance escapes. Macbeth sees Banquo's ghost at the feast. He murders Macduff's family. Each act of violence requires another. Paranoia and isolation deepen. Lady Macbeth descends into guilt-driven madness. |
| TR_N06 | Recognition (Anagnorisis) | "Tomorrow, and tomorrow, and tomorrow..." Macbeth recognizes the emptiness of what he has gained. Life is "a tale told by an idiot, full of sound and fury, signifying nothing." He sees that the witches' promises were traps, and Lady Macbeth's death confirms the total cost. |
| TR_N07 | Downfall (Catastrophe) | Macduff — who was "from his mother's womb untimely ripp'd" — defeats Macbeth in combat. Macbeth dies fighting, his head displayed on a pike. The man who murdered a king is killed by the man whose family he slaughtered. |
| TR_N08 | Aftermath | Malcolm, Duncan's rightful heir, is crowned King of Scotland. Order is restored, but the cost is enormous: Duncan, Banquo, Lady Macbeth, Lady Macduff and her children, Young Siward — all dead because of one man's ambition. |

**Titanic cross-reference:** The elevated state is the Titanic's unsinkable reputation and the upper-class world of first class; the temptation is hubris — ignoring iceberg warnings to maintain speed; the fatal choice is Captain Smith's decision not to slow down; apparent success is the smooth early voyage; unraveling is the iceberg strike and cascading failures; recognition is the moment the crew understands the ship will sink; downfall is the sinking itself; aftermath is the survivors in lifeboats and the inquiry.

**Romeo and Juliet cross-reference:** Elevated state is the passionate love between Romeo and Juliet; temptation is the desire to pursue love despite the family feud; the fatal choice is the secret marriage; apparent success is the wedding night; unraveling is Tybalt's death, Romeo's banishment, and the failed message; recognition is each lover discovering the other's apparent death; downfall is the double suicide; aftermath is the families' reconciliation over the bodies of their children.

## Edge Mappings

| Edge ID | Edge Label | Macbeth Moment |
|---------|-----------|---------------|
| TR_E01 | Flaw Activated | The witches' prophecy targets Macbeth's specific weakness — ambition. The first prophecy's immediate fulfillment makes the greater temptation credible. Lady Macbeth's challenge activates pride alongside ambition. |
| TR_E02 | Crosses the Line | The murder of Duncan. Macbeth deliberates ("If it were done when 'tis done, then 'twere well it were done quickly"), sees the dagger hallucination, and proceeds. The choice is agonized but deliberate. |
| TR_E03 | Reaps the Reward | The coronation. Macbeth gains exactly what the prophecy promised. But the reward is hollow — he cannot sleep, cannot trust, and the crown was purchased with blood. |
| TR_E04 | Cracks Appear | Banquo's suspicion, the need to murder Banquo, Fleance's escape, the ghost at the feast. Each crack requires a worse response. Macbeth's actions generate the very threats he feared. |
| TR_E05 | Sees the Truth | The "Tomorrow" soliloquy. Macbeth confronts the meaninglessness of his achievement. The witches' second set of prophecies, which seemed like protection, are revealed as equivocations. |
| TR_E06 | Falls | Birnam Wood comes to Dunsinane. Macduff reveals he was born by cesarean section. The prophecies' loopholes close. Macbeth fights and dies knowing everything was for nothing. |
| TR_E07 | World Endures | Malcolm's coronation restores legitimate rule. Scotland endures, but the play's final image carries the weight of all who died. The tragedy's lesson — "vaulting ambition, which o'erleaps itself" — echoes beyond the restoration. |
