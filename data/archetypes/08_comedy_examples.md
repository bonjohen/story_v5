# Comedy (Restoration of Order) — Example Mapping

**Primary example:** A Midsummer Night's Dream (Shakespeare comedy)
**Cross-references:** The Big Lebowski (1998 film), Bridget Jones's Diary (2001 film)

---

## Node Mappings

| Node ID | Node Label | A Midsummer Night's Dream Moment |
|---------|-----------|--------------------------------|
| CO_N01 | Constrained Order | Athens under Theseus's law. Egeus demands Hermia marry Demetrius; the law says she must obey or face death or a nunnery. Lysander and Hermia love each other but are forbidden. Helena loves Demetrius, who rejects her. Desire and law are at war. |
| CO_N02 | Disruption of Order | Hermia and Lysander flee into the enchanted forest. Helena follows Demetrius into the same woods. Meanwhile, Oberon and Puck's love-juice scheme introduces magical confusion on top of the human confusion. |
| CO_N03 | Escalating Chaos | Puck applies the love-juice to the wrong Athenian. Lysander wakes and loves Helena instead of Hermia. Oberon corrects by enchanting Demetrius too — now both men pursue Helena, who thinks she's being mocked. Hermia is abandoned and furious. The mechanicals' rehearsal collides with the fairy world, and Bottom gets an ass's head. Titania falls in love with Bottom. |
| CO_N04 | Dark Moment | Helena believes everyone is conspiring against her. Hermia and Helena's friendship shatters. Lysander and Demetrius are about to fight a duel. Real harm — broken friendships, potential violence, total breakdown of trust — threatens beneath the absurdity. |
| CO_N05 | Unmasking and Recognition | Oberon orders Puck to fix the enchantments. The lovers are led through fog, exhausted, and fall asleep. Puck applies the antidote. They wake and discover their true affections restored — Lysander loves Hermia, Demetrius (still enchanted) loves Helena. The confusion is explained as a dream. |
| CO_N06 | Reconciliation | Theseus overrules Egeus — the lovers will marry as they choose. Hermia and Lysander, Helena and Demetrius are paired. Bottom rejoins the mechanicals. The fairy king and queen reconcile. Every broken relationship is repaired. |
| CO_N07 | New Harmony | The triple wedding. The mechanicals perform their play. Oberon and Titania bless the marriages. The rigid Athenian law has been softened, the fairy world is at peace, and the social order is freer and more generous than before. |

**The Big Lebowski cross-reference:** Constrained order is the Dude's lazy routine; disruption is the rug-urinating thugs and the mistaken identity; escalating chaos is the cascading misunderstandings (Bunny's kidnapping, the nihilists, Walter's interventions); the dark moment is Donny's heart attack; unmasking is the revelation that Bunny was never really kidnapped; reconciliation is the Dude's acceptance; new harmony is the Dude abiding.

**Bridget Jones's Diary cross-reference:** Constrained order is Bridget's social world of coupled friends and family pressure; disruption is meeting both Darcy and Cleaver; escalating chaos is Bridget's diary-fueled misreadings of both men; the dark moment is discovering Cleaver's betrayal and believing Darcy despises her; unmasking is reading Darcy's true character; reconciliation is the street-chase and kiss; new harmony is the relationship built on honesty.

## Edge Mappings

| Edge ID | Edge Label | A Midsummer Night's Dream Moment |
|---------|-----------|--------------------------------|
| CO_E01 | Order Broken | Hermia and Lysander's flight into the forest. By leaving Athens, they escape the law but enter a space where different, stranger rules apply. |
| CO_E02 | Confusion Compounds | Puck's misapplication of the love-juice. Each correction creates a new problem — enchanting Demetrius doesn't fix things, it doubles the chaos. The fairy and mortal plots collide through Bottom. |
| CO_E03 | Threatens Real Harm | Helena's genuine anguish at being mocked, Hermia's fury at being abandoned, the men's readiness to duel. The comedy reaches a point where real emotional damage is being done. |
| CO_E04 | Truth Emerges | Puck's fog separates the combatants; the antidote restores Lysander. The lovers wake and can see clearly for the first time. Theseus discovers them and overrides the original decree. |
| CO_E05 | Chooses Forgiveness | Theseus overrules Egeus's demand. The lovers forgive the enchanted night's confusions. Oberon and Titania reconcile after the changeling dispute. Forgiveness flows from authority and from the characters themselves. |
| CO_E06 | Harmony Restored | The triple wedding and the mechanicals' play. The forest's chaos has produced a more generous social order. Puck's epilogue invites the audience to forgive the play itself — extending the comedy's reconciling gesture to the world beyond the stage. |
