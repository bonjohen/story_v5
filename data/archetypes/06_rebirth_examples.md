# Rebirth — Example Mapping

**Primary example:** A Christmas Carol (1843 novella)
**Cross-references:** Groundhog Day (1993 film), Beauty and the Beast (1991 animated film)

---

## Node Mappings

| Node ID | Node Label | A Christmas Carol Moment |
|---------|-----------|------------------------|
| RB_N01 | Fallen State | Ebenezer Scrooge is a wealthy miser who exploits his clerk Bob Cratchit, refuses charity, and scorns Christmas. He is isolated, cold, and universally disliked — powerful in business but dead in spirit. |
| RB_N02 | Warning or Catalyst | The ghost of Jacob Marley — Scrooge's dead partner — appears in chains, warning that Scrooge will share his fate unless he changes. Marley announces three spirits will visit. |
| RB_N03 | Resistance and Denial | Scrooge initially dismisses Marley as "an undigested bit of beef." He tries to sleep through the visitation, bargains with the Ghost of Christmas Past, and rationalizes his choices. His defenses bend but don't break. |
| RB_N04 | Ordeal of Truth | The Ghost of Christmas Yet to Come shows Scrooge his own death — unmourned, his possessions stolen, his name forgotten. Worse, he sees Tiny Tim's empty chair and the Cratchit family's grief. Scrooge confronts the full weight of his wasted life. |
| RB_N05 | Turning Point | Scrooge, weeping at his own gravestone, pledges: "I will honour Christmas in my heart, and try to keep it all the year." He begs the spirit for the chance to change. The choice is explicit, desperate, and total. |
| RB_N06 | Atonement | Christmas morning. Scrooge sends the prize turkey to the Cratchits, donates generously to the charity collectors he previously refused, attends his nephew Fred's dinner, and raises Bob Cratchit's salary. Each act reverses a specific earlier cruelty. |
| RB_N07 | Renewal | Scrooge becomes "as good a friend, as good a master, and as good a man, as the good old city knew." Tiny Tim does not die. The renewal is not a return to a prior self but the emergence of someone Scrooge never was — generous, warm, and connected. |

**Groundhog Day cross-reference:** Phil Connors's fallen state is narcissistic arrogance; the warning is the first repeated day; resistance is his escalating selfishness and nihilism; the ordeal is the realization that nothing he does can save the homeless man; the turning point is his decision to use the loop to help others; atonement is the montage of service; renewal is the morning the loop breaks and Rita is beside him.

**Beauty and the Beast cross-reference:** The Beast's fallen state is his cursed form reflecting his cruelty; the warning is the enchantress's spell and the wilting rose; resistance is his raging temper and isolation; the ordeal is Belle leaving and the castle's despair; the turning point is the Beast letting Belle go; atonement is his willingness to die rather than harm Gaston; renewal is the transformation back — earned through genuine love.

## Edge Mappings

| Edge ID | Edge Label | A Christmas Carol Moment |
|---------|-----------|------------------------|
| RB_E01 | First Crack Appears | Marley's ghost in chains. The crack is supernatural and undeniable — Scrooge's dead partner tells him his current path leads to damnation. |
| RB_E02 | Digs In | Scrooge argues with the Ghost of Christmas Past, deflects the Ghost of Christmas Present's rebukes, and tries to rationalize his life. Each spirit erodes his defenses but he keeps fighting. |
| RB_E03 | Truth Breaks Through | The Ghost of Christmas Yet to Come shows the final consequences: Scrooge's lonely death, the charwoman selling his bedsheets, Tiny Tim's death. The vision is silent, relentless, and inescapable. |
| RB_E04 | Chooses Change | At the gravestone, Scrooge makes his pledge. The choice is active — he doesn't passively accept change but begs for the chance to earn it. |
| RB_E05 | Makes Amends | Each act on Christmas morning directly addresses a prior sin: the turkey for the Cratchits he underpaid, the donation to the collectors he rejected, the visit to Fred he snubbed. Atonement is specific and costly. |
| RB_E06 | Renewal Achieved | Dickens's closing lines confirm the change is permanent. Scrooge keeps Christmas better than any man alive. The renewal is not sentimental — it is structural, changing how Scrooge lives every day. |
