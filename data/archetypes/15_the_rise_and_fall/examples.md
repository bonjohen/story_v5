# The Rise and Fall — Example Mapping

**Primary example:** The Wolf of Wall Street (2013 film)
**Cross-references:** Scarface (1983 film), Citizen Kane (1941 film)

---

## Node Mappings

| Node ID | Node Label | The Wolf of Wall Street Moment |
|---------|-----------|-------------------------------|
| RF_N01 | Humble Origin | Jordan Belfort arrives on Wall Street as a naive, ambitious young broker with a Long Island background and no connections. He is hungry, impressionable, and visibly driven by the desire for wealth. |
| RF_N02 | First Opportunity | After the 1987 Black Monday crash destroys his first job, Belfort discovers penny stocks — high-commission, low-regulation securities sold to gullible investors. The opportunity is legal but ethically bankrupt. His talent for selling is perfectly matched to the scheme. |
| RF_N03 | Rapid Ascent | Belfort founds Stratton Oakmont, a boiler-room brokerage that makes millions through pump-and-dump fraud. He recruits Donnie Azoff and an army of aggressive salesmen. The money arrives in floods — mansions, yachts, drugs, parties. Belfort becomes a Wall Street celebrity. |
| RF_N04 | Peak of Power | Stratton Oakmont at its height: hundreds of brokers, IPOs generating millions, a lifestyle of unchecked excess. Belfort's Forbes magazine profile (intended as criticism) only increases his fame and attracts more recruits. He feels untouchable. |
| RF_N05 | Cracks Form | The FBI, led by Agent Patrick Denham, begins investigating Stratton Oakmont. Belfort's drug addiction spirals. His marriage to Naomi fractures. Employees start cooperating with the government. The empire that was built on manipulation cannot withstand scrutiny. |
| RF_N06 | Desperate Grip | Belfort attempts to hide money in Swiss bank accounts, smuggles cash using couriers, and sails through a storm to reach his offshore accounts. He refuses to take the deal the SEC offers. His drug-fueled decision-making becomes erratic. He nearly kills his daughter while driving high. Each attempt to maintain the empire makes the fall more certain. |
| RF_N07 | The Fall | Belfort is arrested. He cooperates with the FBI and wears a wire against his own employees and friends. He is sentenced to 36 months in federal prison. The empire is gone, the money is gone, and the people who trusted him are prosecuted because of his testimony. |
| RF_N08 | Legacy | Belfort, released from prison, reinvents himself as a motivational speaker — selling his personality to audiences the same way he once sold stocks. The system that allowed Stratton Oakmont continues largely unchanged. The final shot of the audience watching Belfort speak suggests the cycle will repeat: the hunger for easy wealth is not Jordan Belfort's flaw alone. |

**Scarface cross-reference:** Humble origin is Tony Montana as a Cuban refugee in Miami; first opportunity is working for Frank Lopez; rapid ascent is Montana building his cocaine empire; peak is "The World Is Yours" globe in his mansion; cracks form as his paranoia and cocaine use escalate; desperate grip is the war with Sosa; the fall is the final assault on his mansion; legacy is Tony face-down in the fountain — the empire dissolved, the world unchanged.

**Citizen Kane cross-reference:** Humble origin is young Charles Foster Kane inheriting the Inquirer; first opportunity is using the paper to build political influence; rapid ascent is Kane's media empire and political ambitions; peak is his near-governorship and palatial lifestyle; cracks form with the election scandal and Susan's misery; desperate grip is Xanadu — Kane trying to control everything within his shrinking world; the fall is Susan leaving and Kane dying alone; legacy is "Rosebud" — the revelation that what Kane lost first (his childhood) was what he wanted most, and no amount of power could replace it.

## Edge Mappings

| Edge ID | Edge Label | The Wolf of Wall Street Moment |
|---------|-----------|-------------------------------|
| RF_E01 | Seizes the Chance | Belfort discovers penny stocks and realizes his sales talent can generate enormous commissions with minimal oversight. The opportunity is morally compromised from the start — the seeds of the fall are in the first opportunity. |
| RF_E02 | Climbs Fast | Stratton Oakmont's growth. Each successful pump-and-dump reinforces Belfort's methods. Moral compromise becomes routine — defrauding investors is just "business." The lifestyle escalates with the profits. |
| RF_E03 | Reaches the Top | The Forbes profile and the IPOs. Belfort has reached a level where he believes the rules don't apply. His speech to the Stratton Oakmont trading floor — "I'm not leaving" — captures the peak's intoxication. |
| RF_E04 | Erosion Begins | Agent Denham's investigation. The first subpoenas. Naomi's discovery of Belfort's infidelity. The cracks are consequences of the methods: fraud attracts investigators, excess destroys relationships, drugs erode judgment. |
| RF_E05 | Clings to Power | The Swiss bank scheme, the smuggling, the refusal to take the deal. Belfort's attempts to preserve the empire require greater crimes than the ones that built it. The desperate grip accelerates the fall. |
| RF_E06 | Everything Collapses | The arrest and the wire. Belfort's cooperation with the FBI is the final inversion — the man who built his empire on loyalty destroys it through betrayal. The fall is caused by the same talent that enabled the rise: selling. He sells his friends to save himself. |
| RF_E07 | World Moves On | The motivational speaking circuit. The audience's eager faces mirror Stratton Oakmont's trading floor. Belfort is diminished but still performing. The world that produced him continues unchanged — the system is the legacy, not the man. |
