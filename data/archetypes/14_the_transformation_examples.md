# The Transformation — Example Mapping

**Primary example:** Black Swan (2010 film)
**Cross-references:** The Metamorphosis (1915 novella), Spider-Man (2002 film)

---

## Node Mappings

| Node ID | Node Label | Black Swan Moment |
|---------|-----------|------------------|
| TF_N01 | Fixed Identity | Nina Sayers is a technically perfect but emotionally repressed ballet dancer. Her identity is defined by control — of her body, her routine, her relationship with her domineering mother. She is the "good girl," fragile and obedient. |
| TF_N02 | Catalyst of Change | Nina is cast as the Swan Queen in Swan Lake — a role that requires her to embody both the White Swan (controlled, pure) and the Black Swan (passionate, dangerous). Thomas Leroy tells her she is perfect for the White Swan but must find the Black Swan within herself. The role demands a transformation she cannot achieve within her fixed identity. |
| TF_N03 | Denial and Struggle | Nina tries to achieve the Black Swan through more discipline and harder practice — applying her old tools to a new problem. She resists the sensuality and abandon the role requires. She scratches herself compulsively, clings to her mother's control, and rejects Lily's spontaneity. The fixed identity fights to survive. |
| TF_N04 | Liminal State | Nina begins hallucinating — seeing her own face distort, experiencing blackouts, unable to distinguish reality from delusion. She is losing the controlled Nina but has not yet become someone new. Her relationship with her mother fractures. She may be going mad, or she may be transforming. The film refuses to clarify. |
| TF_N05 | Crucible | Opening night of Swan Lake. Nina must perform both swans before an audience. She stabs what she believes is Lily (actually herself) before the Black Swan act. In the performance, she dances the Black Swan with terrifying, ecstatic power — the transformation erupts on stage. She has become what the role demanded, but at the cost of her body and possibly her mind. |
| TF_N06 | Integration | Between the Black Swan act and the final White Swan, Nina discovers the wound is her own. She chooses to perform the final act anyway — the White Swan's death. The integration is not healthy reconciliation but a merging of perfection and destruction. Nina has found a way to be both swans, but only by destroying herself. |
| TF_N07 | New Identity | Nina's final line: "I felt it. Perfect. It was perfect." The new identity is complete — she has achieved the artistic transcendence she sought. But the cost is her life (or sanity). The transformation is fulfilled, but whether it is triumph or tragedy is left for the audience to decide. |

**The Metamorphosis cross-reference:** Fixed identity is Gregor Samsa as dutiful provider for his family; catalyst is waking up as a giant insect; denial is Gregor's attempts to maintain his old routine; liminal state is Gregor's increasing isolation in his room; the crucible is the family's rejection — the apple thrown by his father; integration never comes — Gregor's family cannot accept the transformation; new identity is his death, which paradoxically frees the family. The transformation is entirely external, and the tragedy is the absence of integration.

**Spider-Man cross-reference:** Fixed identity is Peter Parker as a bullied, invisible high school student; catalyst is the radioactive spider bite; denial is Peter's confusion and attempts to understand his powers; liminal state is his early experiments — the costume, the wrestling, the failure to stop the thief; the crucible is Uncle Ben's death and the revelation that the thief was the one Peter let go; integration is Peter accepting that "with great power comes great responsibility"; new identity is Spider-Man — Peter Parker carrying both his ordinary life and his transformed self.

## Edge Mappings

| Edge ID | Edge Label | Black Swan Moment |
|---------|-----------|------------------|
| TF_E01 | Change Begins | The casting as Swan Queen. Thomas's demand that Nina find the Black Swan within herself is the catalyst — the role requires her to become someone she has spent her entire life suppressing. |
| TF_E02 | Resists the Change | Nina practices harder, scratches herself, and clings to her mother's protection. She tries to reach the Black Swan through the White Swan's methods — control, repetition, denial of the body. Every attempt fails because the tools are wrong. |
| TF_E03 | Falls Between Identities | The hallucinations begin. Nina is no longer the controlled ballerina, but she has not become the passionate artist. She exists in a terrifying in-between where reality dissolves and her reflection moves independently. |
| TF_E04 | Forged by Crisis | Opening night. The performance demands the transformation in real time, before an audience. Nina's stabbing of herself and her subsequent Black Swan performance fuse the crisis and the emergence of the new self into a single, violent act. |
| TF_E05 | Integrates Old and New | The decision to dance the White Swan's death scene with the wound. Nina carries the Black Swan's power back into the White Swan's body. The integration is the performance itself — both swans in one dancer, both selves in one person. |
| TF_E06 | Settles Into New Self | "It was perfect." Nina's last words confirm that the new identity — however briefly it exists — is complete. She has transcended the fixed identity, but the cost of transcendence is everything. |
