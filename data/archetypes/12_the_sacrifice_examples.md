# The Sacrifice — Example Mapping

**Primary example:** Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows – Part 2 (2011 film)
**Cross-references:** Armageddon (1998 film), The Dark Knight Rises (2012 film)

---

## Node Mappings

| Node ID | Node Label | Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows Moment |
|---------|-----------|-------------------------------------------|
| SA_N01 | Valued Life | Harry has built a family of choice — Ron, Hermione, the Weasleys, the Order of the Phoenix. He has grown from an orphan under the stairs into a young man with love, purpose, and belonging. Hogwarts is his home. |
| SA_N02 | Threat Emerges | Voldemort's forces attack Hogwarts directly. The Death Eaters breach the castle's defenses. Friends die in the battle — Fred Weasley, Lupin, Tonks. The threat is not abstract: people Harry loves are dying in real time. |
| SA_N03 | Realization of Cost | Snape's memories in the Pensieve reveal the truth: Harry is the final Horcrux. Voldemort cannot be killed while Harry lives. The only way to end the war is for Harry to walk into the Forbidden Forest and let Voldemort kill him. There is no alternative. |
| SA_N04 | Resistance and Struggle | Harry walks through the castle seeing the wounded and the dead. He does not seek an alternative — the resistance is internal, the struggle of a seventeen-year-old boy walking toward his own death. The Resurrection Stone summons his parents, Sirius, and Lupin, who walk beside him. He asks, "Does it hurt?" |
| SA_N05 | The Decision | Harry enters the Forbidden Forest. He does not raise his wand. He stands before Voldemort and accepts the Killing Curse. The decision is total, conscious, and driven by love — not duty, not obligation, but the choice to die so that others may live. |
| SA_N06 | The Sacrificial Act | Voldemort casts the Killing Curse. Harry falls. The Horcrux within him is destroyed. In the liminal space of King's Cross, Dumbledore confirms: the sacrifice worked because Harry chose death freely, just as his mother did for him. Harry's willingness to die protects everyone at Hogwarts. |
| SA_N07 | World Saved | Voldemort is defeated in the final duel — his spells can no longer bind because Harry's sacrifice broke his power. The wizarding world is free. But Fred, Lupin, Tonks, and dozens of others are gone. The epilogue shows Harry nineteen years later — at peace, sending his own children to Hogwarts, living the life his sacrifice made possible. |

**Armageddon cross-reference:** Valued life is Harry Stamper's relationship with his daughter Grace; the threat is the asteroid; the realization is that someone must stay behind to detonate the bomb manually; resistance is the drawing of straws and A.J.'s attempt to take Harry's place; the decision is Harry pushing A.J. back into the shuttle; the sacrificial act is pressing the detonator; the world saved is Earth surviving, Grace marrying A.J., and Harry's photo among the heroes.

**The Dark Knight Rises cross-reference:** Valued life is Bruce Wayne's retirement and relationship with Selina Kyle; the threat is Bane's nuclear bomb in Gotham; the realization is that the bomb cannot be disarmed; resistance is the citizens' failed attempts and Batman's battered condition; the decision is Batman flying the bomb out over the ocean; the sacrificial act is the detonation over the bay (though Wayne secretly survives); the world saved is Gotham rebuilding, with Batman honored as a statue and legend.

## Edge Mappings

| Edge ID | Edge Label | Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows Moment |
|---------|-----------|-------------------------------------------|
| SA_E01 | Danger Arrives | The Battle of Hogwarts. Voldemort's army attacks the one place Harry calls home. Friends begin to die. The danger is immediate and overwhelming. |
| SA_E02 | Cost Becomes Clear | Snape's memories. The revelation that Harry himself is a Horcrux reframes the entire series. Every battle, every friendship, every lesson has led to this: Harry must die. |
| SA_E03 | Fights the Necessity | The walk through the castle. Harry sees the bodies of Fred, Lupin, and Tonks. The resistance is not a search for alternatives — Harry knows there are none. The struggle is the human cost of accepting death at seventeen. |
| SA_E04 | Chooses to Pay the Price | Entering the Forbidden Forest. The Resurrection Stone. Harry's parents tell him they are proud. He drops the stone and faces Voldemort. The choice is made in perfect clarity. |
| SA_E05 | Executes the Sacrifice | Standing before Voldemort without raising his wand. The Killing Curse strikes. Harry's willingness to die for others creates the same protective magic his mother's sacrifice created for him. |
| SA_E06 | The Cost Is Felt | The aftermath of the Battle of Hogwarts. The dead are mourned. Harry breaks the Elder Wand. The wizarding world rebuilds. Nineteen years later, life continues — made possible by a boy who walked into the forest. |
