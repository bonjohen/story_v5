# The Quest — Example Mapping

**Primary example:** The Lord of the Rings (1954 novel)
**Cross-references:** Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade (1989 film), Finding Nemo (2003 film)

---

## Node Mappings

| Node ID | Node Label | Lord of the Rings Moment |
|---------|-----------|------------------------|
| QU_N01 | Home Ground | The Shire — a peaceful, insular community unaware of the growing threat of Sauron. Frodo inherits the Ring from Bilbo. |
| QU_N02 | Objective Defined | The Council of Elrond determines the Ring must be carried to Mount Doom and destroyed. The objective is specific, external, and existential. |
| QU_N03 | Gathering Companions | The Fellowship of the Ring is formed — nine walkers (Frodo, Sam, Merry, Pippin, Gandalf, Aragorn, Legolas, Gimli, Boromir), each with distinct capabilities. |
| QU_N04 | Journey Trials | Caradhras (environmental), Moria (adversarial — the Balrog), Lothlórien (respite and temptation). Each trial escalates and tests the group differently. |
| QU_N05 | Cohesion Test | Boromir succumbs to the Ring's temptation and attacks Frodo at Amon Hen. The Fellowship shatters — the group's deepest fracture is exposed. |
| QU_N06 | Final Approach | Frodo and Sam enter Mordor via the Dead Marshes, Ithilien, and Cirith Ungol. Resources are depleted, the Ring grows heavier, and the landscape is hostile. |
| QU_N07 | Climactic Ordeal | Mount Doom. Frodo claims the Ring for himself. Gollum bites it from his finger and falls. The Ring is destroyed through a confluence of mercy, obsession, and providence. |
| QU_N08 | Objective Won | Sauron is defeated. The objective is achieved — but Frodo is permanently scarred by the Ring's burden. |
| QU_N09 | Homecoming | The hobbits return to the Shire, scour it of Saruman's influence, and restore it. But Frodo cannot find peace and eventually departs for the Grey Havens. |

**Indiana Jones cross-reference:** Home ground is the university; objective is the Holy Grail; companions include Marcus and Sallah; the cohesion test is Indy's father being captured; the climactic ordeal is the three trials in the temple; the objective won is the Grail's healing of Henry Sr.

**Finding Nemo cross-reference:** Home ground is the reef; objective is finding Nemo; companion is Dory; journey trials include jellyfish, whale, and current; the cohesion test is Marlin's argument with Dory; homecoming is Marlin letting Nemo take risks.

## Edge Mappings

| Edge ID | Edge Label | Lord of the Rings Moment |
|---------|-----------|------------------------|
| QU_E01 | Need Emerges | Gandalf identifies the Ring as the One Ring. The Shire — and all Middle-earth — is in mortal danger. |
| QU_E02 | Assembles the Team | The Council of Elrond. Each member volunteers or is appointed, with distinct reasons and capabilities. |
| QU_E03 | Sets Out | The Fellowship departs Rivendell. Frodo's line "I will take the Ring" is the commitment that launches the journey. |
| QU_E04 | Tensions Surface | Boromir's growing obsession with the Ring creates tension throughout the journey, culminating in his attack on Frodo. |
| QU_E05 | Group Recommits | After the Fellowship shatters, Frodo and Sam continue alone. Aragorn, Legolas, and Gimli pursue the Uruk-hai. The quest is now carried by fragments committed to different tasks. |
| QU_E06 | Engages Final Obstacle | The Crack of Doom. Frodo stands at the edge — but the obstacle is the Ring's power over his will. |
| QU_E07 | Claims the Prize | The Ring is destroyed (through Gollum's intervention). The cost: Frodo's innocence and peace of mind. Boromir's death. The destruction of much that was beautiful. |
| QU_E08 | Returns Home | The hobbits return to the Shire, changed. The home they saved is not quite the home they left. |
