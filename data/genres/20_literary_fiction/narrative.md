# Literary Fiction — Narrative Graph Explanation

## Overview

The Literary Fiction depth graph models how the genre's promise of psychological depth, thematic seriousness, and stylistic craft translates into enforceable constraints at every level of creative decision-making. It is designed for writers, editors, and story-evaluation tools to verify that a work of literary fiction delivers on its contract: character interiority rendered with precision, language that carries meaning rather than merely conveying it, and thematic questions held open rather than collapsed into resolution.

The graph flows from a single Genre Promise through four Core Constraints, four Subgenre Patterns, three Setting Rules, and three Scene Obligations, with one Tone Marker and two Anti-Patterns. Each level narrows the creative degrees of freedom, moving from "what the reader expects when they open a work of literary fiction" down to "what must be present and functional in the draft." The defining characteristic of literary fiction is that the journey inward is the story: events happen not to create plot but to give consciousness something to work on.

Unlike genre fiction, where external plot mechanics drive the engine, literary fiction asks the reader to inhabit a particular consciousness — to feel the texture of another person's perception, the distortions of their memory, the gap between what they believe about themselves and what the text makes visible. Every structural decision in this graph, from the level of subgenre down to the level of individual scene, exists to protect and fulfill that fundamental exchange.

## Node Explanations

### Level 1 — Genre Promise

**LF_N01_PROMISE — Literary Fiction Promise**
The root commitment: the reader is promised a story in which the inner lives of characters, the precision of language, and the weight of ideas carry more authority than plot mechanics — a work where meaning accretes slowly and resists easy resolution. In a story, this manifests from the opening pages through deliberate prose choices, a privileged relationship with at least one character's consciousness, and the absence of the urgency-signals that signal genre fiction (threat, countdown, mystery). The reader understands early that the payoff will not be a solved problem but an accumulated understanding. While inside this node, the writer must establish both the quality of attention the prose will sustain and the kind of interiority that will serve as the story's center of gravity.

### Level 2 — Core Constraints

**LF_N10_INTERIORITY — Character Interiority Is the Primary Engine**
The story's momentum comes from what happens inside the character — perception, memory, desire, self-deception, and the slow accumulation of understanding — rather than from external plot events. External events serve the interior, not the reverse. In a story, this appears as sustained passages of thought, memory, and perception that receive the same quality of attention as dramatic scenes; as external events filtered through character consciousness rather than reported neutrally; and as a visible, significant gap between what a character thinks and what they do. While this constraint is active, the writer must ensure that interiority is the narrative's primary forward force — the reader's desire to understand this consciousness is what turns pages, not suspense about external outcomes.

**LF_N11_LANGUAGE — Language and Style Are Expressive Tools**
Prose style is not a neutral delivery mechanism but an active carrier of meaning. Sentence rhythm, word choice, imagery, and structural decisions express character psychology, thematic content, and emotional register — the how of telling is inseparable from the what. In a story, this appears as prose choices that could not be replaced with plainer language without changing the story's meaning; as metaphors that recur and evolve across the text; as sentence structure that enacts emotional states rather than merely describing them. A minimalist literary voice is as purposeful as a lyrical one: the constraint is not ornamentation but intention. While this constraint holds, the writer must ensure every stylistic choice earns its keep — not beautiful for beauty's sake, but precise because precision is how the story thinks.

**LF_N12_THEMATIC_COMPLEXITY — Thematic Complexity Without Easy Answers**
The story engages with ideas — about identity, mortality, memory, justice, love, power, or meaning — in ways that complicate rather than resolve. The work holds contradictions open rather than collapsing them into a thesis. In a story, this appears as characters who are simultaneously right and wrong, as scenes that can be read as supporting opposite interpretations, as an ending that changes the meaning of what came before without explaining it. The reader can articulate the story's central question but not its answer. While this constraint is active, the writer must resist the temptation to let any single scene or character speak for the work's conclusion — every apparent answer should generate a new question.

**LF_N13_AMBIGUITY — Ambiguity Is a Feature, Not a Defect**
The work deliberately withholds certainty — about character motivation, event causality, moral judgment, or meaning — because ambiguity reflects the actual texture of human experience and invites the reader into active interpretation rather than passive reception. In a story, this appears as key events rendered from angles that prevent definitive readings; as a narrator whose reliability is subtly questioned; as character motivations that are plausible in two incompatible ways; as an ending that remains genuinely open to multiple interpretations. The distinction between productive ambiguity and simple vagueness is crucial: ambiguity is purposeful and loaded, whereas vagueness is merely unclear. While this constraint holds, the writer must leave the right things uncertain — not obscuring craft failures but sustaining genuine multiplicity.

### Level 3 — Subgenre Patterns

**LF_N20_PSYCHOLOGICAL_REALISM — Psychological Realism**
Literary fiction grounded in the accurate, detailed rendering of how real human minds work — the irrationality of desire, the distortions of memory, the persistence of trauma, the gap between self-image and behavior — in recognizable social settings. In a story, this appears as characters who believe things demonstrably false about themselves; as memory rendered as selective and constructed rather than accurate; as behavior traceable to prior psychology that has been established. Psychological realism is not therapy-speak: it does not label or explain its characters' inner states but renders them from inside. While in this subgenre, the writer must show inner life operating in real time — its contradictions, its suppressions, its sudden eruptions — rather than summarizing it retrospectively.

**LF_N21_EXPERIMENTAL — Experimental and Postmodern**
Literary fiction that foregrounds and subverts its own conventions — treating form, narrative voice, structure, and the act of storytelling as subjects of inquiry alongside content. The artifice of fiction is visible and intentional. In a story, this appears as structural patterns the reader must assemble; as narrative voice that calls attention to its own construction; as gaps and absences that are purposeful and loaded; as fragmented or non-linear structure that enacts the theme it cannot state. Experimental form is not difficulty for its own sake: the formal choices are legible as meaningful rather than arbitrary, and the work's structure makes an argument that conventional form could not. While in this subgenre, the writer must ensure that every formal disruption is justified by what it makes possible — if the story would be identical told conventionally, the experimentation has failed.

**LF_N22_DOMESTIC_LITERARY — Domestic Literary Fiction**
Literary fiction set within the texture of ordinary domestic life — marriage, family, home, routine — where the apparent smallness of the world makes the interior stakes larger by contrast, and where the symbolic weight of everyday objects and rituals carries the theme. In a story, this appears as recurring domestic objects or rituals that accumulate meaning; as small exchanges that carry disproportionate emotional weight; as the familiar made unfamiliar through the precision of the attention paid to it. The domestic world is not mere setting but an active structure: the house, the dinner table, the garden, the Sunday routine carry the story's content the way a courtroom carries a legal drama. While in this subgenre, the writer must ensure that no domestic detail is merely realistic — everything chosen must also mean.

**LF_N23_SOCIAL_POLITICAL_LITERARY — Social and Political Literary Fiction**
Literary fiction that places individual consciousness in dialogue with systemic forces — history, ideology, class, race, power — without collapsing the personal into the political or using characters as mere representatives of social positions. In a story, this appears as ideological language infiltrating a character's own self-understanding; as systemic forces felt in the body and in daily life, not only described; as the political revealed through private moments rather than public declarations. Characters are individuated people, not social types: the migrant, the class-climber, the subject of empire are rendered as specific consciousnesses shaped by but not reducible to their position. While in this subgenre, the writer must hold both levels simultaneously — the personal must be genuinely personal and the political must be genuinely political, and the story must show how each constitutes the other.

### Level 4 — Setting Rules

**LF_N40_SYMBOLIC_MUNDANE — Mundane Settings Carry Symbolic Weight**
The literal setting — a house, a room, a street, a workplace — functions simultaneously as a plausible physical location and as a carrier of symbolic meaning. The work's thematic content is embedded in concrete particulars rather than stated abstractly. In a story, this appears as descriptions of settings that exceed what narrative economy alone would require; as objects that recur with accumulating significance; as settings that change in ways that track with interior transformation. A decaying house embodies a failing marriage; a meticulously tended garden holds a character's fantasy of control; a city traversed at night maps a psychological state. While this rule is active, the writer must resist the temptation to announce the symbolic register — the reader should feel the weight before they can name it.

**LF_N41_NONLINEAR_TIME — Time May Be Non-Linear or Subjective**
The story's handling of time is shaped by consciousness rather than chronology. Memory, anticipation, association, and trauma may cause time to fold, compress, expand, or loop — because the interior experience of time is not linear, and rendering it truthfully requires non-linear structure. In a story, this appears as present-tense scenes interrupted by memories rendered with equal vividness; as moments the reader understands differently in light of later revelations; as time markers that are absent or unstable. The past in literary fiction is not backstory — it is a living force that exerts pressure on the present. While this rule is active, the writer must ensure that every temporal disruption is motivated by consciousness rather than plot: the character returns to a memory because they cannot escape it, not because the reader needs the information.

**LF_N42_INTERIOR_EXTERIOR — Interior Life Is As Vivid As Exterior Events**
The rendering of a character's inner life — their thoughts, feelings, perceptions, associations, and interpretations — receives the same quality of attention and narrative space as external events. Interior experience is not compressed or summarized. In a story, this appears as prose that slows and expands during moments of heightened perception; as sensory detail filtered through and transformed by character consciousness; as free indirect discourse that blurs the boundary between narrator and character voice; as thoughts that are associative and partial rather than logical and articulate. A minor exterior event opens into extensive interior consequence: what happens outside is less important than how the character is registering it. While this rule is active, the writer must refuse the habit of treating interior life as transition between scenes — it is itself a scene, with duration and texture.

### Level 5 — Scene Obligations

**LF_N60_INTERIORITY_SCENE — Interiority Scene**
At least one extended scene — or a pattern of scenes — that provides deep, unmediated access to the protagonist's inner life: their self-deceptions, contradictions, desires they cannot articulate, and the texture of how they actually perceive their world. In a story, this appears as syntax and diction shifting to track thought rather than action; as associative leaps the character does not explain; as the character noticing things that reveal what they are actually thinking about rather than what they believe they are thinking about. The scene must produce a gap: the reader must know something true about the character's inner life that the character either does not know or cannot say. While in this scene, the writer must abandon articulate self-analysis in favor of rendered consciousness — the character should not deliver insight about themselves but should be seen in the act of not quite knowing who they are.

**LF_N61_EPIPHANY_SCENE — Epiphany or Shift-in-Understanding Scene**
A scene in which a character arrives at a new understanding — of themselves, of another person, of their situation, or of the world — that is earned by everything that has come before. The shift is genuine but not necessarily redemptive; it may be a loss of illusion, a recognition of complicity, or the clarification of a grief that has been unnamed. In a story, this appears as prose slowing at a moment when something seen or heard is processed differently; as repetition of an earlier image or phrase that now carries different weight; as the character doing something small that signals the shift without explaining it. The epiphany must be inevitable in retrospect: the reader should feel that everything in the story was building toward this, even though the moment arrives quietly. While in this scene, the writer must resist the impulse to articulate what has been understood — the understanding should be visible without being stated.

**LF_N62_AMBIGUOUS_ENDING — Ambiguous or Resonant Ending Scene**
The final scene or ending withholds narrative closure in favor of resonance — leaving the reader with an image, a feeling, or an open question that continues to work on them after the text ends. The story does not resolve its tensions so much as find the right note on which to leave them. In a story, this appears as a final scene that is quieter and more condensed than what precedes it; as a single concrete detail carrying disproportionate weight; as a last sentence that could be read as hopeful or devastating depending on the reader. The ending must be specific rather than vague: earned ambiguity is different from trailing off. While crafting this scene, the writer must ask not "what has been resolved?" but "what is the right note on which to leave this character and these questions?" — and then find the concrete image or action that sounds that note.

### Structural Nodes

**LF_N80_CONTEMPLATIVE_TONE — Contemplative, Precise, Emotionally Layered Tone (Tone Marker)**
The dominant tone of literary fiction is one of sustained, careful attention — to language, to inner life, to the weight of ordinary things. It is not detached or cold; it is emotionally available but controlled, allowing feeling to accumulate through precision rather than through declaration. The prose creates space for the reader to feel rather than instructing them what to feel. Pacing is deliberate — scenes have room to breathe. Emotional weight is carried by concrete particulars, not abstract assertions. This tone can accommodate irony, dark comedy, and fragmentation while maintaining overall seriousness; what it cannot accommodate is urgency that overrides reflection, or emotional statement that substitutes for emotional rendering.

**LF_N90_STYLE_WITHOUT_SUBSTANCE — Beautiful Prose Concealing Empty Story (Anti-Pattern)**
Literary fiction fails when stylistic sophistication substitutes for rather than serves psychological depth, thematic seriousness, and human truth. The prose is accomplished but there is no character to care about, no idea being explored, and no felt experience being rendered — only the performance of literary ambition. Sentences can be appreciated in isolation but do not accumulate; no character emerges with interior life despite extensive prose; the reader can admire the writing without being moved by or interested in anything happening in the story. This node is a genre violation: its presence indicates that the story has mistaken its means for its ends. Style must carry something; if nothing is being carried, the style is merely decorating absence.

**LF_N91_PLOTTED_INTERIORITY — Interiority Subordinated to Plot (Anti-Pattern)**
Literary fiction fails when the inner life of characters is treated as connective tissue between plot events — when interiority appears only to explain or react to what has happened externally, and the story's engine is event rather than perception. Interior monologue becomes exposition of what will happen next; emotional states exist to motivate the next plot point rather than as experiences in themselves; interiority is absent from scenes that are not plot-critical. This node is a genre violation: it describes a story that has claimed the literary fiction contract while delivering the genre fiction engine. Characters become agents of a plot rather than consciousnesses that are the story's reason for being.

## Edge Explanations

### Level 1 → Level 2 (Promise to Constraints)

**LF_E01 — Promise requires character interiority as primary engine**: The genre promise triggers the requirement that stakes are interior — understanding, identity, perception — not primarily external. Characters must be rendered from the inside; their consciousness is the narrative's primary subject. Without this edge, the story may claim literary ambition but deliver plot-driven fiction dressed in elevated language. Common failure: interiority treated as backstory that explains plot rather than as the story itself. New constraint introduced: forward narrative momentum must come from what is happening in the character's mind, not from what is happening in the world.

**LF_E02 — Promise requires language as expressive tool**: The promise generates the requirement that the how of telling is a form of meaning-making. Without this edge, prose style can be sophisticated without being expressive — a story of beautiful sentences that could be replaced by plainer ones without changing anything essential. Common failure: ornate language that is interchangeable with plainer prose. New constraint introduced: every significant stylistic choice must carry meaning that would be lost if the choice were made differently.

**LF_E03 — Promise requires thematic complexity without easy answers**: The promise demands that ideas are engaged rather than illustrated. Without this edge, the story has a message rather than a question, and characters are arranged to demonstrate it. Common failure: every element of the story supporting the same clear moral position. New constraint introduced: the story must raise thematic questions it cannot answer, and must resist the desire to resolve its own contradictions.

**LF_E04 — Promise requires productive ambiguity**: The promise establishes that certainty is not the payoff. Without this edge, the story offers the reader the relief of clear answers rather than the more demanding work of interpretation. Common failure: ambiguity introduced early resolved completely by the ending. New constraint introduced: at least one significant element must remain genuinely open — not vague, but purposefully uncertain in ways that reflect the texture of human experience.

**LF_E05 — Promise requires contemplative, layered tone**: The promise signals that the work rewards slow, close reading. Without this edge, prose pace and register signal that readers should move fast, and the interiority that literary fiction requires cannot be established in the time available. Common failure: urgent, plot-driven tone that does not allow interior life to accumulate. New constraint introduced: pacing and register must create space for sustained attention to language and consciousness.

### Level 2 → Level 3 (Constraints to Subgenres)

**LF_E10 — Interiority grounded in realistic psychology (→ Psychological Realism)**: When interiority is the primary engine and the story operates within recognizable real-world conditions, the narrative branches into psychological realism. The triggering condition is the commitment to depicting inner life with clinical and emotional precision — irrationality, self-deception, the persistence of trauma — in settings that are recognizably human. Common failure: psychology is labeled but not rendered. New constraints introduced: characters must believe false things about themselves, and memory must be shown as selective and constructed.

**LF_E11 — Language foregrounded as form becomes experimental**: When language is a primary expressive tool and form itself becomes a subject of inquiry, the narrative branches into experimental and postmodern territory. The triggering condition is the decision to treat the conventions of storytelling as themselves meaningful — to make the structure of the telling part of what the story is about. Common failure: formal experimentation that is purely aesthetic without semantic content. New constraints introduced: structural choices must be legible as meaningful; the form must make an argument that content alone could not make.

**LF_E12 — Interiority situated in domestic life (→ Domestic Literary Fiction)**: When interiority is the primary engine and the setting is domestic or familial, the narrative branches into domestic literary fiction. The triggering condition is the placement of interior experience within the world of home, family, and routine — where the smallness of the world intensifies the stakes of what is felt. Common failure: domestic setting used as mere backdrop. New constraints introduced: domestic details must carry symbolic or thematic weight; the ordinary world must be rendered with a precision that makes it strange and significant.

**LF_E13 — Thematic complexity engaged through social and political forces (→ Social/Political Literary Fiction)**: When thematic complexity is a core constraint and social or political systems are active forces shaping character interiority, the narrative branches into social and political literary fiction. The triggering condition is the decision to show how systemic forces — history, ideology, class, race — constitute and constrain individual consciousness from the inside. Common failure: social forces described from outside rather than felt from inside. New constraints introduced: the political must be felt as personal; characters must be individuated people rather than social types.

### Level 3 → Level 4 (Subgenres to Setting Rules)

**LF_E20 — Psychological realism embeds meaning in the concrete world (→ Symbolic Setting)**: When psychological realism is selected, the narrative gains the setting rule that the external world is a surface on which character psychology is written. What a character perceives and how they perceive it reveals who they are. The transition introduces the requirement that settings and objects be filtered through character consciousness rather than described neutrally. Common failure: settings described accurately but perceived identically by all characters — no filtering through psychology. What often goes wrong: the writer describes a setting carefully but from outside it rather than from inside the character who is experiencing it.

**LF_E21 — Psychological realism demands equally vivid interior and exterior**: When psychological realism is selected, the narrative also gains the rule that interior events — recognitions, denials, shifts in feeling — are as consequential as exterior ones. The transition introduces the requirement that the rendering of consciousness be as detailed and specific as the rendering of event. Common failure: interior life is gestured at through summary while exterior events are rendered fully. What often goes wrong: the writer trusts exterior scenes to carry emotional weight that has not been established through interior rendering.

**LF_E22 — Experimental form frequently disrupts linear time**: When experimental fiction is selected, the narrative gains the setting rule that temporal structure is itself meaningful — when something happened is part of the story's meaning. The transition introduces the requirement that the non-linear arrangement carry semantic content, not just create difficulty or intrigue. Common failure: non-linearity that is decorative — the chronological story would be identical. What often goes wrong: temporal disruptions are used for suspense or mystery rather than for consciousness.

**LF_E23 — Domestic fiction invests mundane detail with symbolic weight**: When domestic literary fiction is selected, the narrative gains the rule that specific domestic objects or routines carry the story's thematic content. The transition introduces the requirement that domestic particulars function on both literal and symbolic levels. Common failure: domestic objects and routines accumulate without any of them acquiring resonance. What often goes wrong: the writer achieves realistic domestic texture but none of the objects or routines become charged with the story's meaning.

**LF_E24 — Social forces are rendered as interior experience**: When social and political literary fiction is selected, the narrative gains the rule that ideological forces must be felt in the body and in daily life rather than merely described. The transition introduces the requirement that the political be lived from inside rather than analyzed from outside. Common failure: social and political forces described from the outside. What often goes wrong: the writer shows a character being affected by social forces without showing how those forces have shaped the character's own self-understanding, language, and perception.

### Level 4 → Level 5 (Setting Rules to Scene Obligations)

**LF_E30 — Symbolic setting occasions deep interior rendering (→ Interiority Scene)**: When symbolic settings are established, they mandate extended scenes of interior rendering — because the character's perception of the meaningful world around them becomes a privileged window into their inner life. The transition introduces the requirement for an interiority scene in which the character's encounter with a symbolic setting generates visible interior consequence. Common failure: symbolic setting is described but no character perceives or responds to it with interiority. What often goes wrong: the writer establishes a symbolically loaded setting and then skips the character's interior response to move the plot forward.

**LF_E31 — Non-linear time creates space for deep interior rendering (→ Interiority Scene)**: When non-linear or subjective time is in use, it mandates that memory and anticipation be rendered with the same vividness as present experience — because all temporal modes are interior events. The transition introduces the requirement for interiority scenes that inhabit multiple time periods simultaneously, showing the character's relationship to time as part of their characterization. Common failure: non-linear structure in which the temporal jumps are plot-motivated rather than consciousness-motivated. What often goes wrong: past sequences function as exposition rather than as living memories that exert active pressure.

**LF_E32 — Equal interiority and exteriority requires dedicated interior scenes (→ Interiority Scene)**: When interior life is rendered as vivid as exterior events, the narrative mandates dedicated interior scenes — because the reader's understanding of character must come primarily from interior rendering, not from what characters do. The transition introduces the requirement that interiority scenes carry the same scene-level attention as action or dialogue. Common failure: interior life exists only in scenes where a character is physically still. What often goes wrong: the writer reserves interior rendering for quiet scenes and defaults to external reporting during action, dialogue, or confrontation, precisely where interior life would be most revealing.

**LF_E40 — Deep interiority prepares the ground for genuine shift in understanding (→ Epiphany Scene)**: When sustained interior rendering has established a character's patterns of thought, perception, and self-deception, it creates the conditions for the epiphany scene — because the shift in understanding is only meaningful against a fully established baseline. The transition introduces the requirement that the epiphany be rooted in the character's specific, established psychology rather than being generic recognition. Common failure: epiphany occurs without the prior interior work that would make it earned. What often goes wrong: the writer provides an epiphany scene that would work for any character in the situation, rather than one that could only happen to this character given exactly what has been established.

**LF_E41 — Shift in understanding leads to resonant but open ending**: When a genuine shift in understanding has occurred, it leads to the ambiguous or resonant ending — because the new understanding must be demonstrated through a final image or action that contains rather than explains what has changed. The transition introduces the requirement that the ending hold the weight of the shift without resolving everything the shift opened up. Common failure: epiphany is followed by resolution that closes all ambiguity. What often goes wrong: the writer, having earned a complex ending, releases the tension by having the character explain or act out what they have understood, converting the resonance into statement.

### Anti-Pattern Edges

**LF_E90 — Language as primary tool risks becoming language as sole content**: When language is elevated but not anchored to character, theme, or felt experience, the story slides from expressive style toward style-as-content — prose that produces admiration without investment. This edge marks the boundary between literary fiction's use of language as a meaning-carrying tool and its failure mode of language as an end in itself. The warning: when sentences can be appreciated in isolation but do not accumulate, the prose has decoupled from its subject and the story has ceased to exist beneath its surface.

**LF_E91 — Interiority as primary engine fails when plot reasserts primacy**: When a story claims interiority as its engine but is actually driven by event, the core literary fiction contract is violated. Interior life becomes instrumentalized — it exists to service plot rather than as the story's subject. Characters become agents of a plot rather than consciousnesses that are the story's reason for being. This edge marks the boundary between literary fiction's use of external events as occasions for interior experience and the genre violation of using interior life as connective tissue between plot events.

## Canonical Walkthrough

**Path: Psychological Realism → Symbolic Setting → Interior Rendering → Epiphany → Resonant Ending**

*Invented story: A middle-aged translator named Marta has spent twenty years translating other people's words and has, she believes, made herself invisible — a transparent medium for meaning. The story opens the week she returns to the city where she left a relationship she has not examined since.*

1. **LF_N01_PROMISE**: The opening pages establish the literary fiction contract through their quality of attention: a slow, precise account of Marta arriving at a rented apartment, noticing the particular light in the windows, noticing that she has opinions about the furniture. The prose is unhurried. There is no external threat or urgency. The reader understands that meaning will accrete, not arrive.

2. **LF_N10_INTERIORITY** (via LF_E01): From the first pages, the story's engine is revealed as interior. When Marta walks through the city, the narrative is primarily concerned with what she perceives and what her perceptions reveal — not what happens to her. A street she avoids without knowing why. A café she enters. The city is a map of her inner life and we are learning to read it.

3. **LF_N11_LANGUAGE** (via LF_E02): The prose renders Marta's relationship to language — she notices idiom, she hears other people's words before she hears their meaning, she thinks in the voices of authors she has translated. Sentence rhythms shift when she is anxious and open when she is at rest. The style is not decoration but diagnosis.

4. **LF_N12_THEMATIC_COMPLEXITY** (via LF_E03): The story's central question emerges gradually: whether disappearing into others' voices, which has been Marta's vocation and her protection, is a form of service or self-erasure — and whether the distinction matters. The story does not answer this question. Scenes accumulate on both sides of it.

5. **LF_N13_AMBIGUITY** (via LF_E04): Marta encounters a woman she once wronged in the course of the old relationship — but the encounter is rendered from Marta's perception, which is unreliable in ways the text signals without stating. What exactly happened twenty years ago? The story offers evidence but not conclusion. The reader cannot be certain whether Marta is a person who failed or a person who believes she failed when she did not.

6. **LF_N20_PSYCHOLOGICAL_REALISM** (via LF_E10): The story operates within recognizable real-world conditions — a European city, a contemporary intellectual's life, the texture of middle age — but its primary interest is the accuracy of Marta's psychology: the specific shape of her self-deception, the way her vocation has served as permission to avoid her own voice.

7. **LF_N40_SYMBOLIC_MUNDANE** (via LF_E20): The apartment Marta has rented is full of the previous tenant's books — books she has not read and cannot read (they are in a language she does not know). The image accretes across the story: these are voices she cannot translate, experiences she cannot mediate, a self she cannot make transparent. The books she cannot translate are what she has avoided in herself.

8. **LF_N42_INTERIOR_EXTERIOR** (via LF_E21): The story's interior and exterior are rendered with equal vividness. A scene in which Marta sits with the woman she wronged over coffee is as much about the coffee — the weight of the cup, the temperature, the strange neutrality of the table between them — as it is about what is being said and enormously more about what is being felt and suppressed and almost said and swallowed.

9. **LF_N60_INTERIORITY_SCENE** (via LF_E32): The story's central interiority scene occurs when Marta, alone in the apartment, attempts to translate a sentence she finds in one of the unknown-language books — not because she understands the language but because she needs to do something with her hands. The scene is a long, associative passage in which what she invents as translation reveals what she believes about her own life. Her invented translations grow more and more like confession. The reader sees what Marta cannot say.

10. **LF_N61_EPIPHANY_SCENE** (via LF_E40): The epiphany is not announced. Marta is in the middle of a conversation — ordinary, unremarkable — when she hears herself use a phrase she has translated many times from another author and realizes she has been quoting someone else's understanding of her own experience for twenty years. The shift is visible in a single action: she stops speaking mid-sentence, and for the first time she does not finish the sentence in someone else's words. The prose is quieter here than anywhere in the story.

11. **LF_N62_AMBIGUOUS_ENDING** (via LF_E41): The story ends with Marta leaving the apartment for the last time, returning the key. She passes the books she cannot read. This time she runs a finger along the spines — not reading, not translating, just touching. Whether this gesture means she has made peace with untranslatability, or whether she is saying goodbye to another set of voices she will not inhabit, or whether the gesture means nothing and she is simply leaving, the story does not say. The reader carries the question out of the text.

12. **LF_N80_CONTEMPLATIVE_TONE** (throughout): The tone throughout is precise and emotionally available without being declarative. The story trusts its images. It never tells the reader how to feel about Marta. It makes Marta vivid enough that feeling becomes unavoidable.

## Variant Walkthroughs

### Variant 1: Experimental Fiction — Fragmented Self-Portrait

A novel in fragments: a contemporary novelist named Daniel has been trying to write the same book for fifteen years. The novel consists of the fragments he has accumulated — opening pages of abandoned novels, notes, failed scenes, memories rendered as fiction, correspondence — arranged not chronologically but by a logic that becomes visible only at the end. The experimental form (LF_N21) makes the story's form its content: the inability to finish the book is the same as the inability to arrive at a coherent self. Non-linear time (LF_N41, via LF_E22) means each fragment's meaning shifts depending on which other fragments surround it; the reader assembles Daniel differently on each pass. The interiority scenes (LF_N60) are the abandoned openings themselves — each is Daniel's consciousness in a particular year, visible in the syntax he chose. The epiphany (LF_N61) arrives in a late fragment, a note to himself, which the reader recognizes as the moment when Daniel understood what the book was — even as he wrote it as a note rather than as fiction, because he could not yet do it as fiction. The ending (LF_N62) is the final fragment: a first sentence, dated to the present day. Whether it is the beginning of the book that will finally be written or another failed attempt, the reader cannot know.

### Variant 2: Domestic Literary Fiction — A Marriage at the End of Something

Two people — Ana and Tom — have been married for thirty-one years. The novel takes place over a single week in which nothing extraordinary happens: they cook, they sleep, they argue mildly, they watch a documentary, they attend a neighbor's retirement party. The domestic literary fiction subgenre (LF_N22) means the mundane details carry disproportionate symbolic weight (LF_N40, via LF_E23): the meal Ana prepares from a recipe she has made so often she no longer measures; the corner of the kitchen Tom has been meaning to repaint for eleven years; the particular way they stand at the sink together without touching. Interior life is rendered as vivid as exterior event (LF_N42): the chapter in which Ana rinses plates while Tom tells a story he has told before is almost entirely interior — what Ana is hearing, what she is not hearing, what she is noticing about her own capacity to be absent while present. The interiority scene (LF_N60) is a long passage in which Ana realizes she has been waiting for something to happen in the marriage without being able to name what she has been waiting for. The epiphany (LF_N61) is small and devastating: she realizes she has been happy. The ending (LF_N62) is the two of them at the table after dinner, the retirement party behind them, a silence neither of them breaks, the corner of the kitchen still unpainted. Whether the marriage will last, whether it should, whether happiness is the right word for what Ana has named — the story ends before any of these questions can be answered.

### Variant 3: Social and Political Literary Fiction — Inside an Inherited Language

Yasmin is the granddaughter of North African immigrants in France, and she has become a professor of French literature — which means her professional identity is constituted by the language that once administered her grandparents' colonization. The social and political literary fiction subgenre (LF_N23) means systemic forces are active as interior forces (LF_N42, via LF_E24): Yasmin thinks and feels in French, and the story renders the specific texture of how the language that is hers is also not entirely hers — the moments when she reaches for a phrase and finds herself in someone else's inheritance. Thematic complexity (LF_N12) is sustained through a story that refuses to resolve whether Yasmin's fluency represents survival, complicity, transformation, or dispossession — and proposes, with evidence, all four readings simultaneously. The interiority scene (LF_N60) occurs when Yasmin reads a student's paper that argues, in excellent academic French, that certain texts cannot be understood by readers who have not lived in a particular way — and Yasmin, reading, feels both the argument's correctness and its damage to her simultaneously. The epiphany (LF_N61) is not a political recognition but a personal one: she is teaching a passage she loves to a student who clearly also loves it, and she is happy, and the happiness is real, and the happiness is complicated, and she does not need to resolve the complication to experience the happiness. The ending (LF_N62) is Yasmin alone in her office, re-reading the passage that has been her anchor for twenty years, and finding that it means something new — not more or less, but differently — and not being able to say yet what that is.

### Variant 4: Psychological Realism — The Failure of Witness

Marcus was present at an accident — a child hit by a car — when he was seventeen. He did not cause it; he did not fail to prevent it; he was simply there. The novel, set thirty years later, is about what it has cost him to have witnessed something he could not have changed. Psychological realism (LF_N20) demands that his psychology be rendered with precision rather than labeled: he is not traumatized in a clinical sense, he has not been diagnosed, he has simply been living for three decades with the specific weight of having been in the wrong place and carried it home. Non-linear time (LF_N41) means the past intrudes on the present in the texture of his perception: what Marcus sees in his seventeen-year-old son, in a particular quality of afternoon light, in the way a car slows before an intersection. The symbolic setting (LF_N40) is an intersection near his house he has never used, always rerouting around it without acknowledging he is doing so. The interiority scene (LF_N60) is the first time he drives through it — his son has learned to drive and navigates the intersection without incident — and what Marcus feels is rendered at length, associatively, the past and present overlapping, the boy who watched and the man who has been watching since. The epiphany (LF_N61) is negative: Marcus understands that he has been carrying this not because it was his fault but because he could not bear to live in a world where such things happen without consequence, without someone being accountable, and he has been willing to hold himself accountable for thirty years to avoid that knowledge. The ending (LF_N62) offers no consolation. Marcus drives through the intersection again, alone this time, and the prose is very quiet.

## Diagnostic Checklist

Use this checklist to evaluate whether a draft fulfills the Literary Fiction genre contract. Each item corresponds to a required node or edge in the graph.

- [ ] **Genre promise established through prose quality**: Do the opening pages establish that style and interiority carry authority, rather than signaling plot urgency? (LF_N01_PROMISE)
- [ ] **Interiority is the primary engine**: Does the story's forward momentum come from interior life — perception, memory, self-deception — rather than from external plot events? (LF_N10_INTERIORITY)
- [ ] **Language is expressive, not merely decorative**: Are prose style choices doing work that plainer language could not — carrying meaning that would be lost if rewritten? (LF_N11_LANGUAGE)
- [ ] **Thematic complexity is sustained without resolution**: Does the story raise central questions it does not answer, holding contradictions open rather than resolving them into a thesis? (LF_N12_THEMATIC_COMPLEXITY)
- [ ] **Ambiguity is productive, not vague**: Is at least one significant element genuinely open to multiple interpretations — purposefully uncertain, not merely unclear? (LF_N13_AMBIGUITY)
- [ ] **Subgenre constraints are active**: If psychological realism, is psychology rendered rather than labeled? If experimental, do formal choices carry meaning? If domestic, do ordinary details carry symbolic weight? If social/political, are systemic forces felt as interior experience? (LF_N20–LF_N23)
- [ ] **Settings carry symbolic weight without announcing it**: Does at least one setting function on both literal and symbolic levels, with the symbolic register legible without being stated? (LF_N40_SYMBOLIC_MUNDANE)
- [ ] **Temporal structure is motivated by consciousness**: If time is non-linear, do temporal disruptions follow the logic of memory and association rather than plot convenience? (LF_N41_NONLINEAR_TIME)
- [ ] **Interior life receives as much space as exterior events**: Are thoughts, perceptions, and feelings rendered with the same specificity and duration as scenes and actions? (LF_N42_INTERIOR_EXTERIOR)
- [ ] **At least one deep interiority scene present**: Is there an extended scene providing unmediated access to a character's inner life, including what they cannot say or do not know about themselves? (LF_N60_INTERIORITY_SCENE)
- [ ] **Interiority scene creates a visible gap**: Does the reader know something true about the character that the character does not or cannot say — is the gap between self-understanding and revealed truth legible? (LF_E40)
- [ ] **Epiphany is earned by prior interior work**: Does the shift in understanding arrive as inevitable in retrospect — rooted in exactly this character's established psychology? (LF_N61_EPIPHANY_SCENE)
- [ ] **Epiphany is rendered, not announced**: Does the character's shift become visible through image, action, or altered perception rather than through explicit self-explanation? (LF_E41)
- [ ] **Ending is resonant without being vague**: Does the final scene leave the reader with a specific image, phrase, or open question that continues to work after the text ends — earned ambiguity rather than deliberate obscurity? (LF_N62_AMBIGUOUS_ENDING)
- [ ] **Contemplative tone is maintained**: Does the narrative voice sustain careful, emotionally available attention throughout — allowing feeling to accumulate through precision rather than declaration? (LF_N80_CONTEMPLATIVE_TONE)
- [ ] **Style is not a substitute for substance**: Is the accomplished prose anchored to character, theme, and felt experience — is there someone to care about beneath the language? (LF_N90_STYLE_WITHOUT_SUBSTANCE — absence check)
- [ ] **Plot does not subordinate interiority**: Does interiority appear throughout, including in non-plot-critical scenes, and does it drive the story's energy rather than explaining plot events? (LF_N91_PLOTTED_INTERIORITY — absence check)
