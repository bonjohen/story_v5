# Detective — Narrative Graph Explanation

## Overview

The Detective depth graph models how the genre's promise of watching a skilled investigator crack a difficult case translates into enforceable constraints at every level of creative decision-making. It is designed for writers, editors, and story-evaluation tools to verify that a detective narrative delivers on its contract: a dedicated investigator applies a visible and consistent method to a genuinely challenging mystery, arriving at a solution that is both earned and demonstrably caused by the detective's own work.

The graph flows from a single Genre Promise through four Core Constraints, four Subgenre Patterns, three Setting Rules, and three Scene Obligations, with one Tone Marker and one Anti-Pattern. Each level narrows the creative degrees of freedom, moving from "what the audience expects" down to "what must appear in the draft." The genre's central pleasures — the satisfaction of an ordered mind working against chaos, the double delight of a solution that feels simultaneously surprising and inevitable — depend on every level of this structure holding firm.

The graph's most distinctive feature is its emphasis on causal accountability: the detective's work must not merely accompany the solution but must cause it. Every node and edge in the lower levels exists to enforce this accountability, ensuring that the reader or viewer who follows the detective's method arrives at the revelation with both intellectual satisfaction and the feeling that justice has been done through rigor rather than fortune.

## Node Explanations

### Level 1 — Genre Promise

**DT_N01_PROMISE — Detective Promise**
The root commitment: the audience will experience the satisfaction of watching a brilliant or dedicated investigator crack a case through method and insight — the pleasure of an ordered mind working against chaos, culminating in a revelation that feels both surprising and inevitable. In a story, this manifests as the overall tonal and structural contract: the reader or viewer understands from early signals (a crime or mystery of genuine weight, a detective whose presence immediately suggests the application of skill, an invitation to observe and deduce alongside the investigator) that this story will deliver an earned solution. While inside this node, the writer must establish the foundational investigative premise and signal that the solution will be won, not stumbled upon. A story that promises a detective narrative but delivers passive revelation, random luck, or a case trivial enough to require no real effort has broken this promise before the investigation has properly begun.

### Level 2 — Core Constraints

**DT_N10_CENTRAL_INVESTIGATOR — Dedicated Investigator as Central Character**
A specific, named investigator must function as the story's central consciousness and driving force — the one whose choices, observations, and deductions move the case forward. In the story, this appears as scenes anchored to the investigator's perspective or actions, case briefings and discoveries filtered through this figure, and other characters who defer to or challenge the investigator specifically. While this constraint is active, the writer must resist distributing the detective function across an ensemble: supporting characters may assist and sharpen the detective's thinking, but the primary problem-solving must belong to one person. A case solved by committee, or one in which the investigator is incidental while other characters drive the resolution, violates the genre's central identification structure.

**DT_N11_VISIBLE_METHOD — Investigator's Method Must Be Visible and Consistent**
The detective's approach to solving the case — whether pure logic, acute observation, persistent procedure, gut intuition read through behavioral tells, or social manipulation — must be legible to the audience and applied consistently throughout. In the story, this appears as scenes where the detective explains reasoning to a partner or observer, notices what others miss, or applies a recognizable investigative habit. Method is not merely a character trait to be described in backstory; it must be demonstrated in scene, early and repeatedly. While this constraint holds, the writer must ensure the method creates both advantages and blind spots — a detective whose method is only ever convenient is not a real investigator. A method that changes arbitrarily based on plot convenience, or that is named but never shown in operation, is invisible and therefore absent.

**DT_N12_WORTHY_CASE — The Case Must Be Genuinely Challenging**
The mystery must present a real intellectual and procedural challenge — one that is genuinely difficult, contains genuine misdirection, and requires the full application of the detective's skill and effort to solve. In the story, this appears as dead ends the detective must reverse, false suspects who initially seem compelling, evidence that points in contradictory directions, and at least one moment where the audience is genuinely uncertain about the solution. A case that resolves too easily robs the genre of its core satisfaction, however skillfully the detective may be written. While this constraint is active, the writer must ensure that the investigation encounters real resistance: the culprit is clever, the evidence is ambiguous, or the case carries hidden layers that make the initial framing misleading.

**DT_N13_EARNED_SOLUTION — Solution Must Come from the Detective's Work**
The case's resolution must be directly caused by the detective's investigation — their observations, deductions, interviews, or persistence must provide the essential link that closes the case. In the story, this appears as a solution chain that is fully traceable: the detective gathered evidence A through a specific investigative action, deduced implication B from a specific reasoning step, and reached conclusion C through a specific confrontation or synthesis. Coincidental resolution, unsolicited confession, or deus ex machina is not merely a structural weakness — it breaks the genre contract. While this constraint is active, the writer must audit every step of the solution and ask: could the detective have arrived here without the specific action they took? If yes, the action must be made necessary, or the solution must be rebuilt from a step that is.

### Level 3 — Subgenre Patterns

**DT_N20_CLASSIC_GOLDEN_AGE — Classic / Golden Age Detective**
The detective story as intellectual puzzle: a genius or highly gifted sleuth applies rigorous logical method to a well-constructed mystery, often in a contained social world, with the emphasis on the elegance of the puzzle and the brilliance of its solution rather than on physical danger or moral ambiguity. In the story, scenes feature carefully planted clues that are visible to the reader in retrospect, a detective who delivers a formal solution presentation, and a social world that functions as a closed system of suspects. The tone is controlled, sometimes mannered, and may be playful despite the seriousness of the crime. While in this subgenre, the writer must ensure the puzzle is genuinely elegant — the solution must be accessible to the reader through the available evidence, and the detective's genius must be demonstrated through specific deductions rather than merely asserted through reputation.

**DT_N21_HARDBOILED — Hardboiled Private Eye**
The detective operates in a corrupt, dangerous world where moral compromise is unavoidable and the investigator's personal code — not institutional authority — is the only reliable compass. Danger, violence, and moral complexity replace puzzle elegance as the genre's primary texture. In the story, this appears as first-person narration with a cynical or weary voice, physical danger to the detective that is real and recurring, clients who are morally compromised or dishonest, and a world that resists clean resolution. While in this subgenre, the writer must genuinely test the detective's code: the world is corrupt not merely in atmosphere but in practice, and the detective must choose, at least once, between principle and survival or self-interest. A hardboiled detective who never faces real danger or whose personal code is never genuinely threatened is performing toughness, not embodying it.

**DT_N22_POLICE_DETECTIVE — Police Detective**
The investigator operates within an institutional structure — police department, intelligence agency, forensic unit — and the genre's texture includes procedural realism, bureaucratic constraint, teamwork, and the tension between institutional rules and individual pursuit of truth. In the story, this appears as scenes involving institutional processes (warrants, chain of command, forensic protocols), team dynamics and hierarchy that affect decisions, and moments where the detective must justify methods to superiors or partners. While in this subgenre, the institution must be an active force, not a decorative label: the detective's standing within the institution, the resources it provides, and the constraints it imposes must all materially affect the investigation. A police label without procedural substance is not a police procedural.

**DT_N23_AMATEUR_CONSULTING — Amateur / Consulting Detective**
The investigator lacks official authority and operates from an outsider position — brought in by necessity, self-appointed, or consulted for unique expertise. The outsider perspective allows methods and observations that insiders miss, while creating friction with official channels. In the story, this appears as moments where the detective's lack of authority creates specific obstacles, where official investigators are wrong in ways only the outsider can see, and where the resolution leverages the unconventional perspective as an advantage insiders could not have achieved. While in this subgenre, the writer must make outsider status substantive: the amateur's freedom from institutional assumptions and their unconventional methods must be what solves the case, not merely flavoring an investigation that could have been conducted by any official.

### Level 4 — Setting Rules

**DT_N40_CRIME_SCENE_EVIDENCE — Crime Scene as Evidence Field**
Physical spaces in the detective story must function as evidence fields — environments that contain readable information about what happened. The crime scene is not merely a backdrop but an active element of the investigation, yielding clues through careful observation and analysis. In the story, this appears as the detective moving through space with deliberate, observational attention, environmental details that are noted by the investigator and prove significant, and scene reconstruction — the dramatization of what happened here through the detective's analysis. While this rule is active, the writer must ensure physical evidence contributes meaningfully to the solution: a crime scene visited but yielding nothing, or a mystery solved entirely through testimony with no physical observation, has wasted the setting's evidentiary potential.

**DT_N41_INTERVIEW_DYNAMICS — Interview and Interrogation Dynamics**
The social world of the detective story is populated by people who conceal and reveal information through conversation — witnesses who misremember, suspects who lie, bystanders who know more than they think. The detective's skill in reading and questioning people is as important as reading physical evidence. In the story, this appears as interview scenes where characters attempt to conceal or redirect, where the detective notices what a subject doesn't say as much as what they do say, and where contradictions between accounts are identified and exploited. While this rule is active, at least one crucial piece of information must be obtained through the detective's skill in human interaction — characters who volunteer all relevant information without detective skill being applied have made the interview function expository rather than investigative.

**DT_N42_INSTITUTIONAL_CONTEXT — Institutional Context**
The detective operates within or against systems — legal, social, bureaucratic, or organizational — and these systems shape what the investigator can do, who they can pressure, and what counts as a valid solution. In the story, this appears as the detective being denied access to information or locations due to authority limitations, institutional rules forcing a choice between procedure and truth, or the social position of suspects affecting how aggressively they can be pursued. While this rule is active, institutional factors must materially affect at least one significant investigative decision: a detective who operates in a social vacuum with no institutional friction, or who ignores rules without consequence, has rendered the institution decorative.

### Level 5 — Scene Obligations

**DT_N60_CASE_ASSIGNMENT — Case Assignment / Discovery Scene**
An early scene that formally initiates the detective's engagement with the case — whether through official assignment, being hired, stumbling upon a crime, or being personally compelled to investigate. This scene establishes the mystery's stakes, the detective's relationship to the case, and the initial framing of what must be solved. In the story, a specific question — who did it, where is she, what happened — is placed before the detective; the detective's decision to take the case is dramatized, not skipped; initial information is provided alongside what remains unknown; and the tone of the case is established in terms of stakes, danger level, and personal relevance. While this scene is required, the writer must ensure the initial mystery framing is specific enough to provide investigative direction — a case so vague the detective doesn't know what they are investigating is not yet a case.

**DT_N61_INVESTIGATION_BREAKTHROUGH — Investigation Breakthrough Scene**
A mid-story scene in which the detective achieves a key deduction, discovery, or insight that substantially changes the course of the investigation — not the final solution, but a turning-point revelation that opens new lines of inquiry or reframes the mystery in a significant way. In the story, this appears as a shift in the detective's demeanor as the picture clarifies, previously gathered evidence being recontextualized by the new discovery, and investigation accelerating in a new direction. While this scene is required, the breakthrough must be earned: no prior investigative effort that makes the breakthrough feel like a reward rather than a gift means the story has not done the work to justify the pivot. A breakthrough provided by another character rather than the detective's own work also fails the scene's function.

**DT_N62_SOLUTION_PRESENTATION — Confrontation / Solution Presentation Scene**
The climactic scene in which the detective reveals the solution — naming the culprit, demonstrating the method, and closing the case. This scene must show the detective's work and reasoning, not merely announce a result, and must feel both surprising and retrospectively inevitable. In the story, this appears as the detective walking through the chain of evidence and reasoning, earlier scenes being retrospectively recontextualized as clues that now click into place, and the culprit's response — denial, anger, confession — validating the revelation. While this scene is required, the writer must resist rushing it: a solution announced without demonstrated reasoning, or one that references evidence the audience cannot verify through prior scenes, fails the genre's most fundamental obligation. The audience must experience both the surprise of the answer and the retrospective recognition that the answer was always available.

### Structural Nodes

**DT_N80_INTELLECTUAL_RIGOR — Intellectual Rigor and Methodical Persistence (Tone Marker)**
The detective genre requires a pervasive sense that the world is knowable through careful attention and rigorous reasoning — that chaos can be ordered, truth can be recovered, and method can prevail over confusion. In the story, this appears as the detective's reasoning being presented with care and detail, attention to evidence modeled as genuinely meaningful, and a resolution that confirms the method worked. Tone variants range from cool analytical precision in a genius detective's voice, to dogged unglamorous persistence in a procedural approach, to weary moral seriousness in a hardboiled worldview. Even when the detective struggles and doubts, the narrative must sustain confidence that the investigative process itself has value and dignity — an investigation presented as futile or arbitrary has abandoned the genre's core worldview.

**DT_N90_PASSIVE_DETECTIVE — Detective as Passive Observer (Anti-Pattern)**
The detective genre fails when the investigator's work is essentially passive — when the solution falls into the detective's lap through spontaneous confession, coincidental discovery, or the actions of other characters rather than through the detective's own active investigation and reasoning. In the story, warning signs include: the detective being present when revelation occurs but not having caused it; the solution speech referencing information not gathered through investigative scenes; the audience being unable to trace a causal link from the detective's actions to the resolution; and the detective seeming surprised by a solution they should have been driving toward. This node IS the failure mode — its presence in a narrative represents a genre violation that invalidates the genre promise.

## Edge Explanations

### Level 1 to Level 2 (Promise to Core Constraints)

**DT_E01_PROMISE_TO_INVESTIGATOR — Promise requires a dedicated central investigator**: The genre promise immediately triggers the requirement for one specific investigator who owns the case. Without this edge, the story may feature detection but delivers no single figure the audience can identify with, follow, and root for. All case stakes are filtered through the investigator's personal engagement — when this edge fails, stakes become diffuse and impossible to attach to. Common failure: a case resolved by diffuse ensemble action with no clear lead, or a detective who is present in scenes but incidental to the plot's actual movement.

**DT_E02_PROMISE_TO_METHOD — Promise requires visible investigative method**: The promise generates the requirement that the detective's approach be legible and demonstrated. Without this edge, the detective's superior competence is asserted but the audience has no means to follow the reasoning or feel the satisfaction of watching method work. Audience engagement depends entirely on being able to observe the detective's way of seeing the world. Common failure: method described in backstory or dialogue about the detective, but never shown operating in an actual investigative scene.

**DT_E03_PROMISE_TO_WORTHY_CASE — Promise requires a genuinely challenging case**: The promise creates the requirement that the mystery be substantive enough to test the detective fully. Without this edge, even a skilled detective solving a trivial case produces no satisfaction — the case's difficulty determines how impressive the solution feels. Common failure: a case framed as important but resolved in the first act without meaningful effort, misdirection, or the detective being wrong about anything.

**DT_E04_PROMISE_TO_EARNED_SOLUTION — Promise requires solution earned through detective's work**: The promise establishes that the resolution must be causally traceable to the detective's investigation. Without this edge, the story delivers a solution but not a detective story — any story can end with a crime being revealed, but only a detective story ends with a crime solved through the detective's specific effort. Common failure: coincidental resolution, spontaneous confession unprompted by detective pressure, or a secondary character providing the crucial link.

**DT_E05_PROMISE_TO_TONE — Promise sets tone of intellectual rigor and persistence**: The promise establishes a worldview in which investigation has value and method is trustworthy. Without this tone, even a correctly structured detective plot feels hollow — the audience needs to feel that what the detective does matters, and that the world is the kind of place where careful attention produces truth. Common failure: a cynical or nihilistic framing where the detective's work is presented as ultimately futile or irrelevant to how the case resolves.

### Level 2 to Level 3 (Core Constraints to Subgenre Patterns)

**DT_E10_METHOD_TO_CLASSIC — Logical method formalized as puzzle-solving**: The detective's method, when it takes the form of rigorous logical deduction applied to an elegantly constructed puzzle, branches into the Classic / Golden Age subgenre. The triggering condition is a method that is primarily inferential — reasoning from observed facts to a necessarily true conclusion — operating within a contained social world designed to function as a puzzle. Stakes become intellectual: the satisfaction of a perfectly solved puzzle rather than moral or physical stakes. Common failure: a genius detective applied to a trivially simple puzzle, or deduction scenes that present conclusions based on information the audience was never given.

**DT_E11_METHOD_TO_HARDBOILED — Method expressed through personal code in corrupt world**: The detective's method, when it takes the form of persistence and moral compass rather than pure logic in a morally corrupt and physically dangerous world, branches into the Hardboiled subgenre. The triggering condition is a world where the detective's institutional or social position provides no protection, and where the method is inseparable from character. Stakes become moral and physical: survival and integrity in a compromised world. Common failure: a hardboiled detective in a fundamentally safe and honest world, or a personal code that is invoked but never genuinely tested by a situation where violating it would make the detective's life easier.

**DT_E12_METHOD_TO_POLICE — Method expressed through procedural rigor within institution**: The detective's method, when it includes procedural discipline and institutional accountability, branches into the Police Detective subgenre. The triggering condition is an investigator embedded in a formal law-enforcement or investigative structure whose rules, resources, and hierarchy genuinely shape what the investigation can do. Stakes include both the case outcome and the investigator's standing within the institution. Common failure: a police label attached to what is functionally a lone-investigator story with no procedural substance or institutional constraint.

**DT_E13_METHOD_TO_AMATEUR — Method expressed through outsider perspective and unconventional approach**: The detective's method, when it derives its distinctive value from an outsider vantage point and specialized knowledge unavailable to official investigators, branches into the Amateur / Consulting subgenre. The triggering condition is an investigator without institutional authority whose freedom from official assumptions is precisely what makes them effective. Stakes include both the case and the detective's lack of institutional protection. Common failure: an amateur detective with full institutional access and resources who faces no meaningful friction from their lack of official standing.

### Level 3 to Level 4 (Subgenre Patterns to Setting Rules)

**DT_E20_CLASSIC_TO_CRIME_SCENE — Golden-age puzzle centers on reading physical evidence**: The Classic subgenre triggers the crime-scene-as-evidence-field rule because the intellectual puzzle depends on physical details that are planted, observable, and significant. The detective's observational genius is specifically demonstrated through the crime scene. Common failure: a golden-age puzzle mystery in which the solution depends entirely on witness testimony, with no physical evidence that the detective reads — testimony-only mysteries deprive the observational method of its primary arena.

**DT_E21_HARDBOILED_TO_INTERVIEW — Hardboiled investigation runs on dangerous human encounters**: The Hardboiled subgenre triggers the interview-dynamics rule because the corrupt world the detective navigates is made of dangerous, evasive, or morally compromised people who must be worked for information rather than cooperating readily. Interview scenes in hardboiled fiction carry physical danger as well as informational stakes — a source may be hostile, may be connected to the people threatening the detective, or may need to be persuaded through means that test the detective's ethics. Common failure: a hardboiled detective who gets full cooperation from every subject with no friction, danger, or need for skill.

**DT_E22_POLICE_TO_INSTITUTIONAL — Police procedural is structured by institutional rules and hierarchy**: The Police Detective subgenre triggers the institutional-context rule because the investigator's identity is defined by their place within a system that both enables and constrains the investigation. Institutional rules constrain how the detective can pursue the case — procedural violations have real consequences for the detective's career and for the case's admissibility. Common failure: a police detective who operates without any meaningful institutional accountability, bending every rule without consequence and receiving no friction from the hierarchy.

**DT_E23_AMATEUR_TO_INTERVIEW — Amateur detective relies on conversational skill to compensate for lack of authority**: The Amateur / Consulting subgenre triggers the interview-dynamics rule because, lacking coercive authority, the detective must obtain all information through social skill, personal intelligence, and unconventional approaches. The amateur's approach to interviews reveals their outsider perspective: they may appear harmless or unimportant, allowing subjects to underestimate them; they may have specialized knowledge that makes subjects want to talk; or they may leverage personal relationships unavailable to official investigators. Common failure: an amateur detective who obtains all crucial information without any meaningful social effort or skill applied to resistant subjects.

### Level 4 to Level 5 (Setting Rules to Scene Obligations)

**DT_E30_CRIME_SCENE_TO_ASSIGNMENT — Crime scene discovery initiates detective's case engagement**: The crime scene, as an evidence field, creates the conditions for the case-assignment scene by providing the initial mystery and its first set of clues. The detective's first encounter with the scene establishes their method in action — how they move through the space, what they notice, what they immediately recognize as significant or anomalous. Initial evidence at the scene defines the apparent nature of the case and introduces its first misdirections. Common failure: a case that begins without any scene establishing the crime or its initial physical evidence, leaving the detective's investigation to begin in an informational vacuum.

**DT_E31_INTERVIEW_TO_BREAKTHROUGH — Key interview produces critical investigative breakthrough**: The interview-dynamics rule creates the conditions for the breakthrough scene when a specific human encounter — a witness who contradicts their earlier account, a suspect who reveals knowledge they shouldn't have, a bystander who mentions a detail others overlooked — provides the insight that reorients the investigation. The detective's skill in reading what a subject doesn't say, and in exploiting contradictions between accounts, is vindicated when the breakthrough arrives. Common failure: a story in which all investigation advances through physical evidence and human interaction contributes nothing to the solution — witnesses exist but are never meaningfully questioned.

**DT_E32_INSTITUTIONAL_TO_ASSIGNMENT — Institutional context frames how the case is assigned and defined**: The institutional context shapes the case-assignment scene by determining who brings the case to the detective, what frame is placed around it, and what the detective is officially supposed to find versus what the investigation actually reveals. The institutional framing of the case may differ systematically from the actual truth, creating a tension between what the detective is mandated to establish and what the evidence actually shows. Common failure: a case assigned in an institutional context where the institution's definition of the case never conflicts with or constrains the investigation's actual direction.

**DT_E40_ASSIGNMENT_TO_BREAKTHROUGH — Case engagement drives investigation toward breakthrough**: The case-assignment scene, by establishing the detective's commitment and the mystery's initial framing, creates the obligation of a breakthrough that rewards the investigation's effort. The breakthrough must be proportionate to the investigation's difficulty — arriving too easily undercuts the case's worthiness, while arriving without prior investigative work makes it feel like a gift rather than an achievement. The struggle before the breakthrough reveals the detective's persistence and adaptability. Common failure: a breakthrough that arrives without any prior investigative effort that would make the insight feel like a reward for methodical work.

**DT_E41_BREAKTHROUGH_TO_SOLUTION — Investigation breakthrough leads to solution presentation**: The breakthrough scene, by reorienting the investigation and establishing the path to the solution, creates the obligation of a solution presentation that fully delivers on what the breakthrough promised. The reframing achieved in the breakthrough must be fully illuminated in the solution presentation — everything the breakthrough opened must be closed. Common failure: a breakthrough that reframes the investigation in an interesting direction, only for the solution presentation to ignore what the breakthrough revealed and arrive at the solution through a different mechanism.

**DT_E42_WORTHY_CASE_TO_BREAKTHROUGH — Case difficulty makes breakthrough meaningful**: The case's genuine challenge — its misdirection, complexity, and resistance to easy resolution — is what makes the breakthrough meaningful. A breakthrough in an easy case is not a breakthrough at all; the difficulty of the case proportionally increases the value of the insight achieved. The detective's breakthrough is earned through demonstrated effort against genuine resistance. Common failure: a case framed as difficult but in which the breakthrough requires no real insight or effort — the detective simply looks in the right place at the right time.

**DT_E43_EARNED_SOLUTION_TO_PRESENTATION — Solution earned through detective's work is demonstrated in presentation**: The earned-solution constraint, which requires the solution to be causally traceable to specific investigative actions, must be made visible in the solution presentation: the detective's causal role must be verifiable by the audience through the reasoning chain they walk through. The presentation must make the detective's work the explicit cause of the resolution. Common failure: a solution announced in the final scene without the detective's reasoning being demonstrated — the audience is told the detective figured it out but cannot verify how or why through what they have witnessed.

### Anti-Pattern Edges

**DT_E50_PASSIVE_DETECTIVE_WARNING — Unearned solution violates the detective contract**: This edge connects the earned-solution constraint to the passive-detective anti-pattern, marking the boundary between a functioning detective narrative and a genre failure. When the solution arises without being caused by the detective's investigative work — through coincidence, spontaneous confession, or another character's action — the audience feels cheated: the promise of watching a detective solve a case is unfulfilled, and the detective is rendered decorative. This edge is the prohibition the entire graph is constructed to enforce.

**DT_E51_CLASSIC_DIFFERENTIATES_HARDBOILED — Classic puzzle focus differs from hardboiled moral danger**: This differentiation edge marks the meaningful divergence between two subgenres that share the core constraints but deliver entirely different genre experiences. The classic detective story is an intellectual construction: its stakes are the satisfaction of a perfectly solved puzzle, and its detective is elevated by superior intellect. The hardboiled story is a moral and physical ordeal: its stakes are survival and integrity, and its detective is defined by endurance under pressure rather than elegance of mind. Common failure in hybrid attempts: applying a classic puzzle structure to a genuinely dangerous, morally corrupt world without adjustment — the mannered puzzle tone sits false against real violence — or applying hardboiled atmosphere without genuine danger or moral cost, producing tough-sounding but ultimately safe fiction.

**DT_E52_POLICE_DIFFERENTIATES_AMATEUR — Institutional authority differs from outsider perspective**: This differentiation edge marks the meaningful divergence between the police detective and amateur/consulting detective subgenres. The police detective's authority, resources, and institutional identity are both their advantage and their constraint — they risk their career and institutional standing when they push against the system. The amateur detective's outsider status is both their disadvantage and their specific advantage — they see what insiders cannot, but they have no institutional protection. Common failure in hybrid attempts: a consulting arrangement where the outsider effectively has full institutional resources and authority with no outsider friction, collapsing the meaningful distinction between the two positions.

## Canonical Walkthrough

**Path: Classic / Golden Age — Locked-Room Puzzle to Formal Revelation**

Story: At Ashworth Manor during a weekend shooting party, the host — Lord Edmund Carver — is found dead in his locked study. The only key was on his person. Inspector-at-large Constance Fell, a retired forensic botanist now called on by country constabularies baffled by unusual cases, is asked to examine the scene before the local police make any arrests.

1. **DT_N01_PROMISE**: The opening scene establishes the genre contract. A body in a locked room, a household of suspects who all claim innocence, and the arrival of Constance Fell — who immediately crouches beside the window frame and says nothing for two minutes while running a gloved finger along the sill. The audience understands: method is present, and the case will be solved through it.

2. **DT_N10_CENTRAL_INVESTIGATOR** (via DT_E01): All subsequent scenes are anchored to Fell. The local constable defers to her; the household watches her; even the suspects address their explanations to her specifically. She owns the case.

3. **DT_N11_VISIBLE_METHOD** (via DT_E02): Fell's method is demonstrated in the first examination: she reads the physical environment as a botanist reads a landscape — noting what has been disturbed, what grows where it should not, what is absent that should be present. Her partner, the constable, learns to follow her attention rather than her words.

4. **DT_N12_WORTHY_CASE** (via DT_E03): The case immediately resists simple solution. The room was locked from the inside; the key was in Carver's pocket; the window latch was engaged; three guests had documented grievances with the victim; and the preliminary cause of death appears to be cardiac arrest — no obvious violence. Nothing points clearly anywhere.

5. **DT_N20_CLASSIC_GOLDEN_AGE** (via DT_E10): The investigation operates as intellectual puzzle. Fell plants no red herrings of her own — she merely reads the ones already present. The household is a closed system. Suspects are interviewed in a sitting room; the crime scene is a hermetically sealed study. The tone is precise and controlled even as the subject matter is a man's death.

6. **DT_N40_CRIME_SCENE_EVIDENCE** (via DT_E20): Fell spends three hours in the study. She notes a faint mineral trace along the exterior windowsill that proves significant; she observes the dead man's plant collection, in which one specimen has been watered recently despite being left by the staff; and she establishes through careful measurement that the desk has been moved — slightly, but measurably.

7. **DT_N60_CASE_ASSIGNMENT** (via DT_E30): The formal assignment scene establishes the central question — how did someone kill a man in a locked room with no sign of forced entry or obvious violence — and Fell's commitment to it. She declines the local inspector's offer to make an immediate arrest ("You'll arrest the wrong person; give me until tomorrow evening").

8. **DT_N41_INTERVIEW_DYNAMICS** (via DT_E23, DT_E21): Fell interviews each suspect in turn, appearing to ask about Carver's plants and gardening habits. The interview that matters is with the victim's nephew, who mentions offhandedly that he and Carver had argued about the glasshouse the previous month — then immediately, unconsciously, corrects himself to say the argument was about the estate finances. Fell notes the substitution without commenting.

9. **DT_N61_INVESTIGATION_BREAKTHROUGH** (via DT_E31, DT_E40): Fell returns to the study and cross-references the botanical trace with the estate's grounds register. The mineral residue on the window ledge matches a specific fertilizer compound used only in the glasshouse — which is accessed from the exterior. The locked-room is not a locked room: it is a room that was made to appear locked after entry through the window, using a specialist's knowledge of the mechanism and the physical evidence of an exit carefully obscured. The nephew is a trained horticulturist. The investigation pivots completely: from impossibility to meticulous concealment.

10. **DT_N23_AMATEUR_CONSULTING** (via DT_E13): Fell's outsider perspective — a botanist, not a detective in any official sense — is exactly what the locked-room required. The police read the room as a crime scene; Fell read it as an environment, and the environment told her things the crime-scene frame would have suppressed.

11. **DT_N62_SOLUTION_PRESENTATION** (via DT_E41, DT_E43): Fell assembles the household the following evening. She walks through every step: the mineral trace and what it establishes about entry; the plant that was watered and what that reveals about who had been in the room; the desk movement and how it confirms the concealment of a second key hidden in the plant pot beneath the specimen; the nephew's verbal slip in interview. The nephew — confronted with the chain — does not confess immediately. He asks to see the plant. Fell shows him the soil around the root ball, disturbed by the hidden key's removal. He sits down heavily and says nothing more. The case is closed.

12. **DT_N80_INTELLECTUAL_RIGOR**: Throughout, the tone has communicated that the room was always readable — the truth was always there, waiting for someone who could see it. The resolution confirms the genre's worldview: careful attention and methodical persistence recovered what deliberate concealment attempted to bury.

## Variant Walkthroughs

### Variant 1: Hardboiled — The Corrupt City and the Compromised Client

Ray Solis, a former vice detective who left the force after refusing to bury evidence, now runs a one-room office in a city where the mayor's re-election depends on keeping certain cases quiet. He is hired by a woman who says her sister has disappeared — then discovers the client has been lying about who her sister is and why she vanished.

The investigation (DT_N21, DT_N11) runs on street-level intelligence work: Solis visits clubs, pressures contacts who owe him, and reads the city's human geography with a cynical but accurate eye. Every interview (DT_N41, DT_E21) carries physical risk — a source is beaten before Solis can reach him; a nightclub owner arranges to have Solis followed. The world is corrupt not as atmospheric color but as functional constraint: official records have been altered, the police captain who would normally cooperate has been bought, and the institutional context (DT_N42) means every official channel is compromised. The breakthrough (DT_N61) comes when Solis recognizes, in a photograph taken at a campaign fundraiser, the face of the client's sister standing next to the mayor — understanding in an instant that the disappearance is not a crime but a concealment, and that his client hired him not to find her sister but to establish plausible deniability for the family. The solution presentation (DT_N62) is not an assembly in a drawing room: it is Solis, alone, laying out what he knows to the client, refusing the doubled fee she offers to make him stop. He publishes what he has, not to the police — who are compromised — but to a journalist he trusts. He wins the case and loses the city.

### Variant 2: Police Procedural — Institutional Truth vs. Institutional Pressure

Detective Sergeant Adrienne Moura is assigned to what her lieutenant has already decided is a gang-related homicide in a neighborhood the department has publicly committed to "cleaning up." The institutional frame (DT_N42, DT_E22) is set before Moura arrives at the scene: the narrative is already written, the arrest is expected to be made quickly, and the case is meant to close fast for political reasons. Moura's method (DT_N11) is meticulous evidence-chain work — she builds cases from physical evidence outward, never from conclusion backward. The crime scene (DT_N40) tells her the staging is wrong: the body placement is inconsistent with the supposed shooting location, and the victim's clothing suggests he was not where the witness accounts claim. She begins a parallel investigation, working the evidence while nominally following the official line. The interview breakthrough (DT_N61, DT_E31) comes when she corners a reluctant witness who, given a detailed evidence summary rather than a general question, realizes Moura already knows more than the official investigation does and decides to tell the truth. The solution (DT_N62) is presented not in a drawing room but in a departmental review meeting where Moura presents her evidence chain to a room full of people who preferred the original conclusion. The case closes correctly. Moura is transferred to a different precinct.

### Variant 3: Amateur / Consulting — Specialized Knowledge as Investigative Lens

Dr. Yusuf Abiodun is a forensic accountant retained by a corporate board to review financial irregularities following the sudden death of the company's CFO — officially ruled a suicide. He has no investigative authority and is formally engaged only to audit the books (DT_N23, DT_E13). His outsider perspective is an accounting mind applied to a social world that thinks entirely in human terms: he reads the company's internal financials the way Constance Fell reads an environment, and what he finds in the numbers are absences — transactions that should exist and don't, reconciliations performed with suspicious precision, a pattern of small transfers that individually mean nothing and collectively prove that someone knew exactly when the CFO's attention would be elsewhere. The interviews (DT_N41, DT_E23) are conducted under the pretext of understanding the accounting process; Abiodun appears harmless, specialized, and unimportant to the people he talks to. The breakthrough (DT_N61) comes when he matches the financial pattern to the personnel calendar and realizes the transfer timing corresponds exactly to a specific individual's access schedule — someone who lacked the financial sophistication to realize their operational signature was preserved in the data. The solution presentation (DT_N62) is delivered first to the board, then to the police, through a forensic accounting report that is also, in its final pages, a complete reconstruction of the crime. The auditor was hired to read numbers. The numbers told him everything.

### Variant 4: Classic / Golden Age with a Wrong Turn — The Red Herring Subgenre

An archaeologist is found dead at a dig site in Crete, apparently having fallen from a coastal cliff. Dr. Philippa Osei, a specialist in ancient Mediterranean trade routes who was present at the excavation as a consultant, refuses to accept the accident verdict because she knows the cliff path, knows how experienced the archaeologist was, and knows that two days before his death he had made a discovery he had told no one about. The investigation proceeds through the physical evidence (DT_N40) at the cliff site and the dig itself, through interviews (DT_N41) with the excavation team — an international group of academics with overlapping publication rivalries and funding dependencies — and through the institutional context (DT_N42) of an academic world where provenance, publication rights, and grant attribution are worth more than most people outside it understand. The case's worthiness (DT_N12) is established through genuine misdirection: the most compelling suspect is the archaeologist's long-time colleague, who had motive, opportunity, and a history of professional grievance. Osei pursues this line for most of the investigation before the breakthrough (DT_N61) reveals that the apparent motive was constructed — that the colleague's grievance was genuine but unrelated to the death, and that the real killer is someone whose connection to the discovery is hidden in a provenance record no one but Osei had thought to pull. The solution (DT_N62) is presented to the full team, walking through every false lead and explaining exactly why each one pointed in the wrong direction — and why the truth was visible all along to someone willing to read the archival record.

## Diagnostic Checklist

Use this checklist to evaluate whether a draft fulfills the Detective genre contract. Each item corresponds to a required node or edge in the graph.

- [ ] **Genre promise established**: Does the story open with clear signals that a skilled investigator will solve a challenging case through method, not luck? (DT_N01_PROMISE)
- [ ] **Central investigator identified**: Is there one specific, named detective who owns the case and drives the investigation? (DT_N10_CENTRAL_INVESTIGATOR)
- [ ] **No investigative diffusion**: Does the case avoid being solved by committee or by the uncoordinated actions of multiple characters? (DT_E01_PROMISE_TO_INVESTIGATOR — anti-pattern check)
- [ ] **Method demonstrated in scene**: Is the detective's investigative approach shown operating in at least one early scene, not merely described or asserted? (DT_N11_VISIBLE_METHOD)
- [ ] **Method applied consistently**: Does the detective's method remain consistent throughout, never changing arbitrarily to suit plot convenience? (DT_E02_PROMISE_TO_METHOD)
- [ ] **Case is genuinely challenging**: Does the investigation encounter dead ends, false leads, or genuine misdirection that the detective must work to overcome? (DT_N12_WORTHY_CASE)
- [ ] **Initial conclusions are revised**: Does the detective get something wrong before getting it right? (DT_E03_PROMISE_TO_WORTHY_CASE)
- [ ] **Solution is causally earned**: Can every step of the final solution be traced directly to specific investigative actions the detective took? (DT_N13_EARNED_SOLUTION)
- [ ] **No coincidental resolution**: Does the case avoid resolving through spontaneous confession, coincidence, or another character's unsolicited action? (DT_E04_PROMISE_TO_EARNED_SOLUTION — anti-pattern check)
- [ ] **Subgenre constraints are active**: If Classic, is the puzzle elegantly constructed with planted clues? If Hardboiled, is the world genuinely corrupt and the detective's code genuinely tested? If Police, do institutional rules materially constrain the investigation? If Amateur, does outsider status create specific friction and provide specific advantage? (DT_N20_CLASSIC_GOLDEN_AGE, DT_N21_HARDBOILED, DT_N22_POLICE_DETECTIVE, DT_N23_AMATEUR_CONSULTING)
- [ ] **Crime scene yields evidence**: Does the physical setting contribute meaningfully to the solution, not merely provide backdrop? (DT_N40_CRIME_SCENE_EVIDENCE)
- [ ] **Detective reads the scene actively**: Are there scenes of the detective moving through physical space with observational attention, noting what others miss? (DT_E20_CLASSIC_TO_CRIME_SCENE, DT_E30_CRIME_SCENE_TO_ASSIGNMENT)
- [ ] **Interview dynamics are substantive**: Do interview scenes feature subjects who conceal, evade, or reveal through behavioral tells — not merely volunteer information? (DT_N41_INTERVIEW_DYNAMICS)
- [ ] **Human interaction contributes to solution**: Does at least one crucial piece of information reach the detective through skill in reading people, not only through physical evidence? (DT_E31_INTERVIEW_TO_BREAKTHROUGH)
- [ ] **Institutional context creates real constraints**: Do institutional factors — authority limits, procedural rules, social hierarchy — materially affect at least one significant investigative decision? (DT_N42_INSTITUTIONAL_CONTEXT)
- [ ] **Case assignment scene present**: Is there an early scene formally establishing the detective's commitment to the case and defining the central mystery question? (DT_N60_CASE_ASSIGNMENT)
- [ ] **Investigation breakthrough scene present**: Is there a mid-story scene in which a key deduction or discovery substantially reorients the investigation? (DT_N61_INVESTIGATION_BREAKTHROUGH)
- [ ] **Breakthrough is earned**: Does the breakthrough arrive after prior investigative effort that makes it feel like a reward rather than a gift? (DT_E40_ASSIGNMENT_TO_BREAKTHROUGH, DT_E42_WORTHY_CASE_TO_BREAKTHROUGH)
- [ ] **Solution presentation scene present**: Is there a climactic scene in which the detective names the culprit and demonstrates the reasoning chain? (DT_N62_SOLUTION_PRESENTATION)
- [ ] **Reasoning is shown, not only announced**: Does the solution presentation walk through the chain of evidence and deduction, allowing the audience to verify the detective's work? (DT_E43_EARNED_SOLUTION_TO_PRESENTATION)
- [ ] **Earlier scenes are retrospectively recontextualized**: Does the solution presentation make earlier clues click into place as significant rather than introducing new information? (DT_E41_BREAKTHROUGH_TO_SOLUTION)
- [ ] **Tone sustains confidence in method**: Does the narrative communicate throughout that careful attention and rigorous reasoning can recover truth, even when the detective struggles? (DT_N80_INTELLECTUAL_RIGOR)
- [ ] **Detective is not passive**: Is the detective the active cause of the resolution — not a bystander to a revelation that happens around them? (DT_N90_PASSIVE_DETECTIVE — absence check)
